## Outcome

Continuing from a request to render ADR-059 in simpler language, the product
authority caught that a condensed draft had preserved a partial-supersession
sentence — and that sharpened into a broader ruling and a scope expansion.

**Sharpened principle.** Accepted documents must give a *consistent current view
of the system, not its transformation over time*: the `accepted` set is
self-contained and mutually consistent (the current-system view); superseded
docs and the supersede chains are the transformation view, reachable via
materialized frontmatter edges but never required to read the present. The
**changelog** is the sanctioned exception — the one place an accepted doc names
its superseded predecessor; content sections stay self-contained.

**Scope expansion.** The product authority expanded the work into an
artifact-system restructuring: base-schema additions (three materialized,
gate-enforced bidirectional edge pairs — supersedes/superseded-by,
derives-from/derived-by, references/referenced-by — plus `tags`, `distribution`
{product, system-wide}, external references); breaking the knowledge ADRs/PDRs
down by needs/schema × base/per-artifact; per-artifact writing skills with
shop-templates enforcement; and programmatic nav/render/query tooling.

**Decision — leverage the branch, defer the restructuring.** The migration
branch already put the corpus in largely the needed state, so it does only
**light frontmatter care**; the restructuring is a separate deferred initiative.
The full shaping (north star, decomposition into ordered intents #1–7,
supersession accounting, schema additions, changelog rule) is captured in
`drafts/artifact-system-restructuring.md` and tracked as epic **`lead-uhxoc`**.

**Frontmatter care done this session.** Withdrew (deleted) the two never-ratified
drafts **ADR-067** and **PDR-035**; cleared `sess-2026-07-19-a`'s now-dangling
`produced` edges and annotated it. Coherence gate green in distribution mode
after the withdrawal. `current-state.md`'s four validate failures were left
as-is: they are a typedef-vs-instance conflict (the typedef models current-state
as a versioned, supersede-able artifact — `current-state-NNN`, status
current/superseded, two required sections — while the live doc is a singleton
rewritten in place). That is a per-artifact-schema decision for the restructuring
initiative, not a frontmatter tweak, so it was flagged rather than force-fixed.
