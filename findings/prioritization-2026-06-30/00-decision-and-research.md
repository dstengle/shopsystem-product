# Backlog Prioritization — Decision & Research Process (2026-06-30)

**Chosen frameworks:** A = **WSJF / CD3 (Cost of Delay ÷ Job Size)** · B = **MoSCoW (Must / Should / Could / Won't)**

## Decision report

SCORING BASIS — read against the ACTUAL backlog (50 live beads, not the broken `undefined` target). The registry confirms every characteristic in the brief: (a) dense dependency chains — items are explicitly "GATED on", "follow-up", "blocks", "unblocks", "for <id> dispatch"; (b) cost-of-not-doing varies wildly — adopter-blocking failures (lead-z3e2 fresh-bootstrap regression, lead-x4mk stale starter, lead-lu91/p83k MITM-TLS credential failures) sit beside pure hygiene (lead-glel/rtgg phantom mirror reconciles, lead-kf36 jsonl normalization); (c) much of the work is enabler/contract-artifact shaped — Gherkin scenario pins, ADR-043/PDR-022 authoring, lead-mirror reconciliations — which have NO end-user reach and unlock downstream BC work; (d) effort is uniformly small-to-medium dispatches; (e) a real subset needs human product-authority judgment (lead-cnjj bootstrap security posture [product-decision], lead-vglj product-discovery, the initiative beads).

The decisive selection criterion for THIS context is: which axes actually carry signal for items that are dependency-, risk-, and enabler-shaped rather than customer-feature-shaped? Per the deanpeters when-to-use guidance, the match is to DECISION TYPE and DATA MATURITY, not to a default favorite. Three of the named frameworks (Kano, RICE, ICE) assume a customer respondent and/or a reach number that simply does not exist for a lead-mirror reconcile or a scenario hash re-pin — the eng-backlog research documents this as their common root failure. That pushes them down the ranking regardless of their general popularity.

PRIMARY CHOICE — WSJF/CD3 (fit 9). It is the only mainstream framework whose value term natively scores the two things that dominate this backlog: Risk-Reduction/Opportunity-Enablement (the lever that lets a contract pin, an ADR finalization, or the agent-vault enabler out-rank a flashier item without any user-reach number) and Time-Criticality (which separates adopter-blocking bugs from deferrable hygiene). Crucially, its denominator is Job Size/duration, and dependencies INFLATE duration — so the cheapest-to-unblock items (e.g. resolve lead-ml51 before its gated dependents lead-py3y/scdm/7wta) rise automatically. That is exactly the dependency-aware sequencing this backlog needs. deanpeters guidance maps it cleanly: "sequencing under time pressure → WSJF/CoD; dependency-heavy roadmaps, economic framing." Use the SAFe relative-Fibonacci WSJF variant, not money-based CD3 — there are no delay-cost dollars to model here, so CD3's economic precision is unavailable and unnecessary; WSJF's relative scores are right-sized for this estimation maturity.

COMPLEMENT CHOICE — MoSCoW (fit 7 as a complement). I deliberately did NOT pick a second value/effort scorer (RICE, ICE, or Value-vs-Effort), because all three RE-USE the same value and effort estimates WSJF already consumes — they are coarser projections of the same axis, so pairing one with WSJF yields redundancy, not the "illuminate different axes" the task demands. MoSCoW adds a genuinely ORTHOGONAL axis: a DISCRETE scope/commitment boundary. WSJF produces a continuous ranking but never draws a line and never flags which items belong to the human product-authority. MoSCoW's signature moves supply exactly what WSJF hides: (1) an explicit Must cap that forces the adopter-blocking subset (z3e2, x4mk, lu91, p83k) to be named non-negotiable for the next bootstrap/adopter release; (2) an explicit Won't-this-time bucket that legitimizes deferring phantom-mirror and jsonl hygiene instead of letting them churn; (3) a stakeholder-alignment round-trip that is the natural home for the genuine product-judgment items (cnjj security posture, vglj discovery) — which in this shop's own rules is the admissible "scope or vocabulary" escalation trigger. deanpeters maps it precisely: "fixed deadline or fixed scope negotiation; aligning stakeholders on a release boundary," plus heuristic #6 "separate strategic vs tactical," #7 "scoring narrows the list, judgment makes the final call," and #8 "numeric scores are inputs to a conversation, not the verdict." The operating pattern: WSJF orders the whole dependency-laden list; MoSCoW draws the release line across that order and surfaces the human-authority decisions.

WHY THE PAIR BEATS THE RUNNERS-UP. What WSJF misses, MoSCoW covers: no scope boundary, no explicit deferral, no stakeholder/human-judgment surfacing, and a false-precision risk on soft Fibonacci scores — MoSCoW is the communicable cut and the judgment conversation that tempers the math. What MoSCoW misses, WSJF covers: MoSCoW has no economic or dependency model, no ordering within a bucket, and drifts toward "everything is a Must" — WSJF supplies the dependency-unblock-first sequencing and the within-Must ordering, and its RR/OE term keeps enablers from being dismissed as Could-haves. Runners-up rejected: Value-vs-Effort (6) and RICE (5) duplicate WSJF's value/effort axis and RICE's Reach is meaningless for internal/enabler items; Eisenhower (5) overlaps WSJF's time-criticality and is gut-feel/not-value-aware; Weighted Scoring (6) could subsume WSJF but only by re-deriving it at higher ceremony cost; ICE (4) adds no axis WSJF doesn't already cover; Kano (2) and Opportunity Scoring (3) require a customer respondent / discovery posture this execution backlog lacks. The WSJF+MoSCoW pair therefore covers both the continuous economic-sequencing axis AND the discrete scope/human-judgment axis with minimal overlap — the cleanest complementary span available for this backlog.

## Framework ranking (fit 0-10 for this context)

### WSJF / CD3 (Cost of Delay ÷ Job Size) — 9/10
Strongest primary fit. RR/OE numerator term is the only built-in handle for the enabler/contract-artifact and risk-burndown items that dominate this backlog (Gherkin pins, ADR-043/PDR-022, agent-vault substrate) — they score on risk-reduction/future-enablement, not user reach. Time-Criticality cleanly separates adopter-blocking bugs (z3e2, x4mk, lu91/p83k) from deferrable hygiene. The Job-Size denominator folds dependencies in: cheap-to-unblock items (resolve lead-ml51 before its gated dependents) rise automatically — exactly the dependency-aware sequencing this list needs. Caveat: use the relative-Fibonacci WSJF variant, not money-based CD3 (no delay-cost dollars to model here); guard against false precision in soft Fibonacci scores. deanpeters: 'sequencing under time pressure / dependency-heavy roadmaps / economic framing.'

### MoSCoW (Must / Should / Could / Won't) — 7/10
Best COMPLEMENT, not a primary. Weak as a 50-item ranker (no economic or dependency model, Must-drift) — but scoped to the release-boundary cut it adds the one axis WSJF lacks: a discrete scope/commitment line. Must cap forces the adopter-blocking subset to be named non-negotiable for the next bootstrap release; explicit Won't-this-time legitimizes deferring phantom-mirror/jsonl hygiene; the stakeholder-alignment round-trip is the home for the genuine human-product-authority items (cnjj security posture, vglj discovery) that WSJF's math would otherwise bury. deanpeters: 'fixed scope negotiation; aligning stakeholders on a release boundary.'

### Value vs Effort (2x2 quadrant) — 6/10
Fast lightweight triage and the eng-backlog research endorses a risk×cost-to-fix quadrant variant as a debt/bug supplement. But it largely RE-USES the value and effort estimates WSJF already consumes — a coarser projection of the same axis, so pairing it with WSJF yields redundancy, not a new axis. Its one-dimensional 'value' misses urgency and dependencies. Useful as a cheap first-pass sanity sort, not as the complementary second framework.

### Weighted Scoring / Weighted Decision Matrix — 6/10
Flexible enough to encode RR/OE, dependency cost, and adopter-impact as explicit weighted criteria — could in principle subsume WSJF. But that is precisely the problem: it re-derives WSJF at higher ceremony, requiring stakeholder agreement on weights for a backlog of mostly small dispatches. Reserve for high-stakes contested calls; here WSJF is the purpose-built, lighter specialization. deanpeters: 'reserve heavy scoring for high-stakes or contested calls.'

### RICE (Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort) — 5/10
Usable only with heavy adaptation. Reach/Impact are built around end-user audience size, which is meaningless for internal/enabler/contract items (no users to 'reach' for a lead-mirror reconcile or scenario re-pin) — research explicitly flags tech-debt and internal initiatives as poorly addressed. Ignores dependencies and time-criticality, the two axes that matter most here, and risks false precision. Centercode/Medium comparisons land on WSJF over RICE for exactly this case.

### Eisenhower Matrix (Urgent × Important) — 5/10
Decent tactical triage of the live bug/ops queue and protects Quadrant-2 strategic initiatives (agent-vault, iterative-experimentation) from being starved by urgent firefighting — relevant to a router operating a live shop. But it is a time/task tool, not value- or dependency-aware, its labeling is gut-feel, and its urgency axis overlaps WSJF's Time-Criticality. Adds little WSJF doesn't already capture more rigorously.

### ICE (Impact × Confidence × Ease) — 4/10
RICE-lite, fast triage when reach data is absent. But no Reach term, all three factors gut-scored and gameable (esp. Confidence), and it adds no axis that WSJF or a Value/Effort pass don't already cover. Best for ranking many cheap growth experiments — which this dependency-laden execution backlog is not.

### Opportunity Scoring (importance vs satisfaction) — 3/10
A discovery-phase lens for finding under-served user outcomes. This is an execution/sequencing backlog, not a discovery one; it would apply only to the single discovery item (lead-vglj), not the 50-item ranking. Wrong phase, wrong unit.

### Kano Model (Must-be / Performance / Delighter) — 2/10
Poorest fit. Requires a customer functional/dysfunctional survey, but internal contract/enabler/bug items have no customer respondent. Measures satisfaction, not cost-of-delay, dependency, or risk. Research documents Kano as a poor fit for internal eng backlogs — it would degenerate into a wishlist with no signal for risk-burndown or dependency-unblocking.
