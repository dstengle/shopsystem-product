## Source (pre-modernization)

#### Context

PDR-013 framed a real, operator-cited pain: BC internal design quality is
poor (6k-line implementation files; work broken down into layer- or
task-shaped units rather than thin vertical slices). Its problem-framing
pass deliberately declined to converge on the *owner* of design quality —
challenging the assumption that the make-it-pass Implementer is the obvious
owner (PDR-013 framing Assumption B) — and routed the role question here, to
`lead-cnbu`. The decomposition/right-sizing *discipline* shipped role-agnostic
(features/templates/154–158, `lead-5hm1`); this ADR decides **whether a new
role owns design quality, and what that role is.**

The operator's locked intent (`lead-ir9m`) is two-sided and must be honored
exactly: **yes** a BC-local architect, **no** a BC-local PO. The asymmetry is
principled and is the load-bearing distinction this ADR pins: *design ≠
product intent.* Product intent forking into the BC is rejected; structural /
design judgment localized to the BC is adopted.

This is a structural decision about the BC-shop role catalogue with no
product-UX surface change to the framework's outward face — hence an **ADR**,
not a PDR.

##### Pre-state empirical findings (artifact surface, ADR-018 D1/D2)

Verified from the lead CWD on 2026-06-10 against the spec sections, ADR/PDR
records, and bead registry in this repo. No BC implementation read, run, or
git-observed; the lead host carries no `repos/` BC source (ADR-018).

1. **§4 (BC-shop) names exactly two roles today — Implementer and Reviewer —
   and NO architect. CONFIRMED.** `04-bc-shop.md` §4.1 defines only the
   Implementer (make-it-pass: receive work, break down, implement, run BDD,
   report) and the Reviewer (adversarial gate: probe against scenarios,
   construct failing cases, block `work_done`, propose tightenings).
   `grep -in "architect" 04-bc-shop.md` returns no role definition. So the
   BC-local architect is **genuinely a new role** on the spec surface, not an
   unpinned existing one.

2. **The two existing BC postures are both downstream of design. CONFIRMED**
   (read of §4.1/§4.2). The Implementer's stated posture is *make-it-pass*
   ("the Implementer's job is to make things work"); the Reviewer's is
   *adversarial-after-the-fact* ("the Reviewer's job is to find where they
   break"). Neither posture is *design-sensibility-up-front*. §4.2's "Break
   down into sub-issues" is an Implementer activity, and §4.1's
   silent-inference clause says the criteria for a good breakdown live in the
   *role template*, not the spec. So there is a posture seam — "is this the
   right structure, decided before code" — that no §4 role currently holds.

3. **Product intent is constitutionally a lead-shop concern; the §4.4 loop
   keeps it there. CONFIRMED** (read of §3.1, §4.4). §3 (lead-shop) places
   the PO as the sole owner of product intent and the named party for scope /
   vocabulary `clarify`. §4.4's loop is *Reviewer-proposes-tightening →
   `clarify` → lead PO decides → `request_bugfix`*: the BC never decides
   requirements; it proposes, the lead decides. A BC-local PO would fork that
   authority into the BC, contradicting the §4.4 mechanism and §3's PO
   ownership. **This is why there is no BC-local PO** — it is not an omission,
   it is a constraint the spec already enforces.

4. **The decomposition discipline that shipped is role-agnostic and awaits an
   owner. CONFIRMED** (read of PDR-013 §3 / `lead-5hm1` close note,
   features/templates/154–158). The shipped scenarios pin the *discipline*
   (thin vertical slices, forbid layer/task-shaped units, split
   multi-When/Then units) and explicitly leave *which BC role owns it* to
   `lead-cnbu`. So this ADR has a concrete first responsibility to assign to
   the new role.

5. **@scenario_hash retirement enumeration. CONFIRMED — empty.** This ADR
   authors no Gherkin and retires/supersedes no pinned `@scenario_hash`
   coverage. `grep -r "@scenario_hash" features/` over the lead-held tree
   surfaces no scenario this decision contradicts; the role-introduction is a
   spec/template structural decision, and the template realization (a follow-up
   dispatch, see Consequences) is purely additive against §4.2. **There is no
   conflicting BC `@scenario_hash` set to enumerate or retire.**

#### Decision

##### D1 — Introduce a BC-local **architect** role into the §4 BC-shop role catalogue, with a design-sensibility-up-front posture distinct from both existing roles

The BC-shop role catalogue (§4.1) gains a third role: the **BC-local
architect**. Its posture is **design sensibility up front** — *decide the
internal structure of the assigned work before (and as) it is built*. It is
the role that holds "is this the right decomposition / boundary / structure?"
which finding 2 establishes neither existing role holds.

The three BC-shop postures are now distinct and sequential-with-overlap:

| Role | Posture | When it acts | Question it owns |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BC-local architect** | design-up-front | before / as work is structured | *is this the right internal structure?* |
| **Implementer** | make-it-pass | building | *does the code make the scenarios pass?* |
| **Reviewer** | adversarial-after | gating | *where does it break / what isn't pinned?* |

The architect is **upstream of the Implementer's make-it-pass** and
**complementary to the Reviewer's adversarial-after** — design quality is
decided up front (architect) and defended at the gate (Reviewer), rather than
left ownerless between them (the PDR-013 seam).

##### D2 — The BC-local architect's activities

The BC-local architect owns, at minimum:

1. **Own the decomposition / right-sizing discipline** (the `lead-5hm1`
   discipline, finding 4): decide that assigned work is broken into thin
   vertical slices each pinning one coherent behavior; forbid layer- and
   task-shaped units; split multi-When/Then units. The Implementer still
   *opens* the breakdown beads (§4.2), but the *right-sizing judgment* is the
   architect's — this resolves PDR-013's deferred role-ownership question.
2. **Decide internal structure and boundaries** of the BC's implementation
   (module/component boundaries, where a behavior lives), so the periodic
   reader and the Reviewer face behavior-shaped units, not a monolith.
3. **Record BC-local design decisions as BC-local ADRs**
   ([ADR-035](035-three-tier-adr-hierarchy-and-periodic-system-architect-review-cadence.md)
   tier 3) — the structural intent the periodic reader needs.
4. **Escalate STRUCTURE questions to the lead architect**, and **escalate
   SCOPE/INTENT questions to the lead PO** via the existing `clarify` vehicle
   — the BC-local architect decides *how it is built*, never *what is built*
   (D3).

##### D3 — There is NO BC-local PO; the BC-local architect MUST NOT decide product intent

Product intent stays at the lead (finding 3, §3.1 / §4.4). The BC-local
architect's authority is **structural / design only**. It MUST NOT author or
fork requirements, redefine scope, or decide vocabulary — those remain lead-PO
concerns, reached from the BC via `clarify` exactly as the §4.4 loop already
routes Reviewer-found tightenings. The discriminator the role applies, on every
judgment: *is this a question of how the work is built (mine) or what the work
is (the lead PO's)?* This keeps the §4.4 mechanism intact: the BC proposes and
the lead decides on intent; the BC decides autonomously only on structure.

##### D4 — The role realization lands in the BC-shop role templates, NOT in a new spec activity beyond the role introduction

Per §4.1's silent-inference clause (finding 2) and PDR-001 decision #2, the
role's *operating criteria* (posture, activities, the structure-vs-intent
discriminator, sufficiency markers) live in the **BC-shop role template**
(`bc-architect.md`), iterable without spec churn. The §4 spec change is
minimal: name the third role in the §4.1 catalogue and add its rows to the
§4.2 activity table. The substance is template content. This mirrors how the
lead-shop roles work (a one-line spec catalogue entry, a role-complete
template per PDR-001).

#### Alternatives considered

**Option A — Make the Implementer own design quality (bake the owner into the
make-it-pass role).** Rejected — this is PDR-013 framing Assumption B
(premature convergence). The make-it-pass posture is *why* monoliths ship
(finding 2); loading design-up-front onto the same role that is optimizing for
"does it pass" re-creates the ownerless seam under a different name. Design
sensibility is a distinct posture and deserves a distinct role.

**Option B — Make the Reviewer own design quality.** Rejected — the Reviewer's
posture is adversarial-*after* (finding 2); design decided only at the gate is
PDR-013's rejected S1c (push the cost to the gate; the monolith is already
built). The Reviewer remains the *backstop* (it can still find "this unit
bundles unrelated behaviors"), but the discipline belongs *upstream* of the
gate.

**Option C — Add a BC-local PO alongside the BC-local architect (symmetry with
the lead's PO+Architect pair).** Rejected (D3) — and rejected upstream by
`lead-ir9m` decision (1). Product intent forking into the BC contradicts §3.1
(PO owns intent) and the §4.4 loop (BC proposes, lead decides). Design ≠
intent: the asymmetry (architect yes, PO no) is the principled result, not an
oversight.

**Option D — No new role; enrich the Implementer template with the
decomposition discipline only (the `lead-5hm1` slice as the whole answer).**
Rejected — `lead-5hm1` deliberately shipped the discipline *role-agnostic* and
routed the ownership question here. Leaving it owner-implicit re-buries the
PDR-013 seam (finding 4); the operator's locked intent explicitly calls for
the role (`lead-ir9m` decision 2).

#### Consequences

- **§4 of the spec gains a third BC-shop role** (BC-local architect) in the
  §4.1 catalogue and §4.2 activity table — a minimal spec edit (D4); the
  substance is template content.
- **A new `bc-architect.md` role template is implied** in the
  `shopsystem-templates` BC, role-complete per PDR-001 (posture, the D2
  activities, the D3 structure-vs-intent discriminator). **This is a follow-up
  `assign_scenarios` dispatch to `shopsystem-templates`, flagged NOT dispatched
  here** (the templates BC is flaky/offline per `lead-yxsr`; it already holds a
  held dispatch queue with features/templates/146–158). The PO authors the
  pinning Gherkin; the Architect dispatches once the templates BC is online.
- **The `lead-5hm1` decomposition discipline now has an owner** (D2.1): the
  BC-local architect. The held features/templates/154–158 dispatch can, when
  the templates BC comes online, be re-grounded so the discipline's *owner* is
  the bc-architect role rather than role-agnostic — a PO/Architect follow-up,
  not actioned here.
- **No BC-local PO is or will be introduced** (D3); product intent stays at the
  lead, the §4.4 loop is unchanged.
- **BC-local design decisions get a recorded home** (D2.3) — the BC-local ADR
  tier defined in
  [ADR-035](035-three-tier-adr-hierarchy-and-periodic-system-architect-review-cadence.md).
- **No Gherkin authored, no dispatch sent, no `@scenario_hash` retired** here
  (finding 5).
