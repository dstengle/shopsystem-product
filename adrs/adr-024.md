## Context

Reviewing the shipped lead-5p07 journal (lead-fm9x) surfaced a day-one
false-negative avalanche: the seven MVP scenarios are strictly
`work_done`-EVENT-driven, so on day one every BC journal + lead snapshot is
empty and the entire already-built corpus shows as OUTSTANDING. The fix is a
missing primitive — a BC (re)builds its AUTHORITATIVE journal from its
as-committed `@scenario_hash`-tagged `features/` tree, idempotently — plus a
proposed sc06 predicate refinement. The PO authored both. This ADR records the
ownership/discriminator call for sc08 and the adjudication of sc06.

Pre-state empirical findings (artifact surface, ADR-018 D1/D2), verified
2026-06-08 from the lead CWD against the lead-held `features/`, the installed
`scenarios`/`shop-msg` contract tools, and `shop-msg` mailbox/registry state —
no BC implementation read, run, or git-observed. **(1)** No
bootstrap/rebuild/backfill primitive exists on the contract surface —
CONFIRMED: `scenarios --help` subcommands are `hash, verify, list, count,
titles, tags` (it enumerates and hashes but holds no journal and no
populate-journal-from-features op); `shop-msg --help` subcommands are
`respond, send, nudge, read, pending, consume, sweep, promote, prime, dump,
watch, bc-status, registry` (no `journal`/`rebuild`/`bootstrap`/`backfill`);
the journal capability landed via lead-5p07 (ADR-023 D2/D3) is event-driven
only (the journal appends off `work_done` ingest of `scenario_hashes`, with no
rebuild-from-committed-features path) — so sc08 is genuinely new, the Q1 fact
that resolves the discriminator to `assign_scenarios`. **(2)** sc08 and the
revised sc06 hashes verify, piping each bare block through the installed
`scenarios hash` (the admissible ADR-018 D2 run): sc08 → `d1f0cf457019db29` ✓
(matches its `@scenario_hash:` tag); revised sc06 body → `5174e405a19358fa` ✓.
**(3)** @scenario_hash retirement enumeration (the message-type / supersession
pre-state step): `grep -r "@scenario_hash" features/scenario-journal/` over the
lead-held tree, each hash recomputed via `scenarios hash` — the old sc06
`f58a7dc39c4e718a` is the only entry the revised body would retire-and-replace,
and it is pinned in `shopsystem-scenarios` (the lead-onfq dispatch carried
`f58a7dc39c4e718a`, its `work_done` demonstration reported
`scenario_hashes: [f58a7dc39c4e718a]`, bead metadata
`scenario_hashes_pinned: f58a7dc39c4e718a`, dispatch_state=closed); sc08 itself
retires nothing (purely additive), so the only retirement in play belongs to
the sc06 question, adjudicated in D2. **(4)** The landed sc06 implementation
already keys outstanding on journal-absence, NOT on work_done history —
CONFIRMED from the `work_done` demonstration: the lead-onfq `work_done`
(scenarios) states "with a record present, the recorded block-only hash is
correctly excluded from outstanding while the unrecorded one remains, and
denominator counts both," so the realized predicate is
`enumerate(features) − recorded_set` where `recorded_set` is the journal, and
the "no work_done ever landed" conjunct in the old Gherkin prose was descriptive
scaffolding, never the operative computation. **(5)** Committed-vs-lead-held sc06
drift risk — CONFIRMED and resolved: `git show HEAD:.../06...` is the OLD body
`f58a7dc39c4e718a` (committed under f510099, matching what scenarios pinned),
and the PO's revision to `5174e405a19358fa` was an uncommitted working-tree
modification — leaving it would drift the lead-held canonical from the
BC-committed pin — reverted (D2).

Two alternatives were considered and rejected. (a) Dispatch the revised sc06 as
`request_bugfix` with explicit retirement-naming of `f58a7dc39c4e718a` —
rejected (D2): the outcome change is a no-op against the landed
journal-absence-keyed implementation, and the revised predicate's "lead
snapshot" conjunct straddles the scenarios/messaging seam, so a clean
single-owner retire-and-replace is unavailable without re-opening or splitting
the seam — not worth a round trip for a defensive restatement. And splitting
sc08 across `scenarios` (enumerate) + `messaging` (populate) as two dispatches —
rejected (D1): messaging already composes the scenarios enumeration via the
in-place `lead-9b3w → lead-onfq` dep, sc08 is one operation on the journal store,
and splitting manufactures a seam ADR-023 already drew.

## Decision

**D1 — sc08 is a new op ON the `shopsystem-messaging`-owned journal store; one
`assign_scenarios` dispatch to messaging, riding ADR-023 D2.** The discriminator
resolves to `assign_scenarios` (finding 1: net-new, no unpinned existing
behavior). Ownership is `shopsystem-messaging` — the journal store is
messaging's (ADR-023 D2: "append-only store of completed block-only hashes per
BC"), and sc08's rebuild is a new operation that populates that store from the
BC's as-committed `@scenario_hash`-tagged `features/` tree. The "which tagged
scenarios are present" enumeration is a `scenarios`-owned primitive (ADR-023 D1)
that messaging already composes: the lead-9b3w (messaging journal store) →
lead-onfq (scenarios enumeration) dependency is already in place and both are
closed, so sc08 creates no new scenarios→messaging seam — it reuses the
established one. A single dispatch to messaging is the coherent vehicle;
splitting sc08 across two BCs would manufacture a seam ADR-023 already drew once.
lead-7hsl is the fresh lead bead carrying sc08 as `work_id`, wired
`lead-fm9x → lead-7hsl → lead-9b3w` via `bd dep`.

**D2 — The sc06 predicate-tightening is DEFERRED as marginal; sc06 reverted to
its landed body `f58a7dc39c4e718a`; tightening tracked as a follow-up.** The
revised sc06 (`5174e405a19358fa`) drops the "no work_done ever landed" conjunct
and adds an "absent from the lead snapshot" conjunct. Against the artifact
surface this is marginal, not load-bearing: the landed sc06 implementation
already keys outstanding on journal-absence (finding 4), so the dropped conjunct
was never operative — the day-one false-negative the umbrella bead flags is
caused by an empty journal, not by the sc06 predicate, and sc08 (D1) fixes that
feedstock by populating the journal from committed features, so once sc08 lands
`enumerate − recorded` yields the correct outstanding set with no change to
sc06's implementation; and the added "lead snapshot" conjunct concerns the
lead-facing view (ADR-023 D3, lead-if3j, messaging) whereas the old sc06 tag
lives in `scenarios` (the enumeration source, lead-onfq), so a clean
single-owner retire-and-replace `request_bugfix` is not available (it would
either re-open the scenarios/messaging seam or split one tightening across two
BCs incoherently), and since the snapshot mirrors the journal (ADR-023 D3) the
conjunct does not change the landed outcome — it is the defensive/explicit
restatement the stakeholder accepted as consistent (per the resolved
snapshot-vs-journal authority question, ADR-023 D3). Therefore option (b): sc06
is reverted to the landed, BC-committed body `f58a7dc39c4e718a` (eliminating the
finding-5 drift), and the predicate tightening is tracked as a follow-up to
revisit only if a concrete false-positive/negative is demonstrated after sc08
lands; dispatching it now as a retire-and-replace would cross the seam
incoherently for a no-op outcome change.

**D3 — Bootstrap ≡ the ADR-023 secondary-job-a failure-recovery rebuild (one
operation serves both).** sc08's operation — populate the authoritative journal
from the as-committed `@scenario_hash`-tagged features tree, idempotently — IS
the agent-failure-recovery rebuild ADR-023 deferred (secondary job a, the reason
the BC journal is authoritative). One operation serves both bootstrap (day-one
backfill of the already-built corpus) and recovery (rebuild after journal loss).
The bootstrap-completion predicate is `@scenario_hash` tag committed +
demonstrated green in the BC's gated commit-time state — NOT "a work_done
landed" (sc03's append trigger). Idempotency is folded into sc08 as an And-step
(rebuild twice ⇒ identical entry-by-entry), not a separate scenario. The
lead-side bootstrap needs no new lead primitive: it rides sc05
(`d0a74c6e8ecb8eb3`) — pull each now-populated BC journal.

## Consequences

- One additive `assign_scenarios` dispatch (lead-7hsl → `shopsystem-messaging`),
  on-wire `scenarios[].hash == d1f0cf457019db29` confirmed by read-back. No
  `request_bugfix`/`request_maintenance`; no `@scenario_hash` retirement (sc08 is
  additive; the sole retirement candidate, old sc06, is deferred not dispatched).
- The lead-held canonical `features/scenario-journal/06` is back to
  `f58a7dc39c4e718a`, in lockstep with the scenarios-committed pin — no
  lead-held-vs-BC-committed drift.
- Once sc08 lands and BCs rebuild, the false-negative avalanche resolves: the
  journal carries the already-built corpus, sc05 bootstraps the lead snapshot,
  and outstanding = `enumerate − recorded` is correct without re-dispatching
  sc06.
- The bootstrap/recovery unification means the deferred secondary-job-a workflow
  is satisfied by the same primitive — no separate recovery dispatch is owed.
- Related surface (bare IDs): ADR-023 (the decomposition this rides — D1/D2/D3 +
  secondary job a); ADR-018 (the artifact-surface evidence rule the findings
  honor); `features/scenario-journal/08` (sc08, `d1f0cf457019db29`), `…/06`
  (reverted to `f58a7dc39c4e718a`), and `…/03,04,05` (the journal/snapshot
  scenarios sc08 + sc05 compose over); and the lead-fm9x umbrella, lead-7hsl sc08
  dispatch, with lead-onfq/lead-9b3w/lead-if3j (closed) journal units.

#### Decision

##### D1 — sc08 is a new op ON the `shopsystem-messaging`-owned journal store; one `assign_scenarios` dispatch to messaging, riding ADR-023 D2

The discriminator resolves to `assign_scenarios` (finding 1: net-new, no
unpinned existing behavior). **Ownership: `shopsystem-messaging`** — the
journal store is messaging's (ADR-023 D2: "append-only store of completed
block-only hashes per BC"), and sc08's rebuild is a **new operation that
populates that store** from the BC's as-committed `@scenario_hash`-tagged
`features/` tree. The "which tagged scenarios are present" enumeration is a
`scenarios`-owned primitive (ADR-023 D1) that messaging **already composes**:
the `lead-9b3w` (messaging journal store) → `lead-onfq` (scenarios enumeration)
dependency is already in place and both are closed. sc08 therefore creates **no
new scenarios→messaging seam** — it reuses the established one. A single
dispatch to messaging is the coherent vehicle; splitting sc08 across two BCs
would manufacture a seam ADR-023 already drew once.

`lead-7hsl` is the fresh lead bead carrying sc08 as `work_id`, wired
`lead-fm9x → lead-7hsl → lead-9b3w` via `bd dep`.

##### D2 — The sc06 predicate-tightening is DEFERRED as marginal; sc06 reverted to its landed body `f58a7dc39c4e718a`; tightening tracked as a follow-up

The revised sc06 (`5174e405a19358fa`) drops the "no work_done ever landed"
conjunct and adds an "absent from the lead snapshot" conjunct. Against the
artifact surface this is **marginal, not load-bearing**:

- The landed sc06 implementation already keys outstanding on journal-absence
  (finding 4), so the dropped conjunct was never operative. The day-one
  false-negative the umbrella bead flags is caused by an **empty journal**, not
  by the sc06 predicate — and sc08 (D1) fixes that feedstock by populating the
  journal from committed features. Once sc08 lands, `enumerate − recorded`
  yields the correct outstanding set with **no change to sc06's
  implementation**.
- The added "lead snapshot" conjunct concerns the **lead-facing view**
  (ADR-023 D3, `lead-if3j`, messaging), whereas the old sc06 tag lives in
  **`scenarios`** (the enumeration source, `lead-onfq`). A clean single-owner
  retire-and-replace `request_bugfix` is not available: it would either re-open
  the scenarios/messaging seam or split one tightening across two BCs
  incoherently. The snapshot mirrors the journal (ADR-023 D3), so the conjunct
  does not change the landed outcome — it is the defensive/explicit restatement
  the stakeholder accepted as consistent (per the resolved snapshot-vs-journal
  authority question, ADR-023 D3).

**Therefore: option (b).** sc06 is reverted to the landed, BC-committed body
`f58a7dc39c4e718a` (eliminating the finding-5 drift), and the predicate
tightening is tracked as a follow-up to revisit only if a concrete
false-positive/negative is demonstrated after sc08 lands. Dispatching it now as
a retire-and-replace would cross the seam incoherently for a no-op outcome
change.

##### D3 — Bootstrap ≡ the ADR-023 secondary-job-a failure-recovery rebuild (one operation serves both)

sc08's operation — populate the authoritative journal from the as-committed
`@scenario_hash`-tagged features tree, idempotently — **is** the
agent-failure-recovery rebuild ADR-023 deferred (secondary job a, the reason
the BC journal is authoritative). One operation serves both bootstrap (day-one

loss). The bootstrap-completion predicate is **`@scenario_hash` tag committed +

lead-side bootstrap needs **no new lead primitive**: it rides sc05
(`d0a74c6e8ecb8eb3`) — pull each now-populated BC journal.
