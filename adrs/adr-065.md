## Source (pre-modernization)

#### Context

David raised this 2026-07-06 after a live failure in the same session: the
first architect pre-state finding reported fabro's *current* state wrong
because it read `findings/fabro-spike/` and stale ADR-048 forward-looking
prose ("not yet an owned BC", "beyond the spike") as authoritative — when
fabro was in fact already productionized (`bc-container launch
--orchestrator fabro`, pinned in `features/shopsystem-bc-launcher/`). Spike
material is a **context liability** if it is not contained: it reads exactly
like a current-state record (dated, detailed, empirically grounded at the
time it was written) but is a *snapshot*, not a *standing* fact, and this
repo had no rule making that distinction load-bearing at read time.

##### Pre-state empirical findings (against the artifact/contract surface, ADR-018)

No BC code read or run; no `repos/` on this host. Established from this
repo's own `findings/`, `adr/029`, `adr/030`, `adr/032`, and `lead-4khi`'s
own evidence:

- `findings/` mixes throwaway/graduated spikes with **active** initiatives
  and durable discipline notes, with **no lifecycle marking distinguishing
  them**: `dummyco-spike-iter-2.md` … `iter-7.md` (throwaway, WS-0/PDR-018
  MVP-gate iterations, epic `lead-jdfb`), `fabro-spike/` (18 files,
  graduated to ADR-048/050/051), `dagger-spike/` (13 files, graduated to
  ADR-052/053/054/055), `agent-vault-credential-spike.md` (graduated to
  ADR-045/049), `fabro-2pc-as-steps-spike.md` and
  `substrate-candidate-comparison-vs-fabro.md` (both informed ADR-048's
  fabro-adoption decision) sit alongside `bc-lifecycle/`,
  `bc-workloop-single-source/`, `progressive-disclosure/`,
  `scenario-integrity/`, `ddd/` (active initiatives) and durable notes
  (`architect-prestate-verification-discipline.md`,
  `scenario-supersession-and-dispatch-discipline.md`,
  `venv-install-hygiene-and-fix-tooling-discipline.md`,
  `provision-template-value-format-probe-discipline.md`) with no marker
  distinguishing any of these categories at a glance.
- **ADR-030's own "Consequences" section names this exact open question**
  and defers it: "the findings-doc *post-graduation* lifecycle … frozen
  historical vs. a `superseded by ADR-NNN` header … recommend the
  supersession-header pattern when PO authoring or a follow-up ADR closes
  it." No follow-up ADR has closed it until now.
- **ADR-029 stage 6 ("Teardown")** names findings-doc survival as the
  spike's only durable output but does not name what happens to that
  document's *disclosure* once the spike's initiative ends (graduates or is
  abandoned) — it is silent on whether the doc keeps living on the active
  read surface indefinitely.
- **No precedence rule exists today.** Nothing in `adr/029`, `adr/030`, or
  `adr/032` states that a spike/findings document is subordinate to
  ADR/PDR content on conflict, or that pre-state verification (ADR-018)
  must not treat a findings doc as current-state evidence. The live
  failure this ADR responds to is exactly that gap.

#### Decision

##### D1 — Hard precedence rule: spike/findings material is never authoritative over ADR/PDR

On any conflict between `findings/` material (including spike docs) and
`adr/`, `pdr/`, `features/`, or the live CLI/mailbox surface, the
**ADR/PDR/features/live-surface wins**. A `findings/` document records what
was true *at the time the spike ran*; it is never re-validated as the
initiative around it moves, so it degrades to historical record the moment
a decision graduates past it. Concretely, for **pre-state verification**
(ADR-018 D1/D2): `findings/` material is **not** part of the admissible
contract/artifact-surface evidence set for establishing current BC/lead
state. It may be read for *background/rationale*, never cited as the basis
for a "the BC currently does X" or "the product is currently shaped Y"
claim. Where a `findings/` doc and an ADR/PDR appear to disagree, the
disagreement is resolved in the ADR/PDR's favor without further
investigation — the findings doc is stale by construction, not a competing
source of truth.

##### D2 — Spike lifecycle has exactly two end-states; a spike outside an active effort MUST be archived

Every spike/findings document reaches one of two end-states, no other:

1. **Graduate** — the spike's finding is codified as an ADR (and, where
   behavior is pinned, Gherkin scenarios via the normal PO/architect
   dispatch path). The findings doc is not deleted — deleting it would
   erase the experimental provenance an ADR's "Alternatives considered" or
   "Pre-state empirical findings" section may cite — but it is marked
   **superseded** (D3) the moment the ADR lands, closing ADR-030's deferred
   question with the supersession-header pattern ADR-030 recommended.
2. **Archive** — the spike did not graduate to a standing decision (a
   dead-end hypothesis, a throwaway validation iteration, an evaluation
   that fed a decision made and recorded elsewhere). It is moved to the
   archival home (D3) as soon as its parent initiative is no longer active.

A spike/findings document that is not part of an active effort and carries
neither a graduation marker nor an archive marker is a **lifecycle
violation** — the same class of gap that produced the fabro
mis-verification this ADR responds to. The check "is every non-active
`findings/` doc graduated-and-marked or archived?" is mechanical and can be
scenario-pinned (PO's job, not decided here).

##### D3 — Archival home is out of the active-read context surface

The archival home is **`findings/archive/`**, a subdirectory excluded from
the default active-read surface (an architect or PO doing routine pre-state
verification does not walk into `findings/archive/` unless a bead or ADR
explicitly directs it there for historical rationale). Concretely:

- A single-file spike doc moved to archive gets a one-line header prepended:
  `> **ARCHIVED** — historical spike record, not current state (ADR-065).
  Superseded by: <ADR-NNN | "informed decision recorded in ADR-NNN" |
  "no graduation — dead end">.`
- A multi-file spike directory (e.g. `fabro-spike/`, `dagger-spike/`) gets
  the same header prepended to its synthesis/index file (the `00-*-recon.md`
  or equivalent entry point); the directory moves as a unit.
- Active initiatives (`bc-lifecycle/`, `bc-workloop-single-source/`,
  `progressive-disclosure/`, `scenario-integrity/`, `ddd/`) and durable
  discipline notes are **not** archived by this ADR — D2 only reaches
  material whose parent effort has actually ended. Whether/when an active
  initiative later closes and its findings archive is a future call, not
  made here.

##### D4 — This ADR's own landing performs the first sweep

Applying D2/D3 to the pre-state evidence above, this ADR's landing moves
the following to `findings/archive/`, each with the D3 header: the six
`dummyco-spike-iter-{2..7}.md` throwaway MVP-gate iterations (initiative
concluded — WS-0/PDR-018 MVP gate closed in later iterations, no further
spike work is pending under `lead-jdfb`), `fabro-spike/` (graduated —
`ADR-048`, `ADR-050`, `ADR-051`), `dagger-spike/` (graduated — `ADR-052`,
`ADR-053`, `ADR-054`, `ADR-055`), `agent-vault-credential-spike.md`
(graduated — `ADR-045`, `ADR-049`), `fabro-2pc-as-steps-spike.md` and
`substrate-candidate-comparison-vs-fabro.md` (both informed the fabro-adoption
decision recorded in `ADR-048`, no dedicated graduation ADR of their own).
This is a mechanical application of D2's criteria to already-cited evidence,
not a fresh judgment call over material outside that evidence set — active
initiatives and durable notes are untouched.

#### Alternatives considered

**Option A — Delete graduated/superseded spike docs instead of archiving.**
Rejected. An ADR's "Alternatives considered" or "Pre-state empirical
findings" section sometimes cites the originating spike doc directly (e.g.
ADR-048 cites the fabro-spike findings); deleting the doc breaks that
citation trail. Archive-with-header preserves provenance while removing the
doc from the active-read surface.

**Option B — A frontmatter status field (`status: archived`) instead of a
directory move.** Rejected for now. A directory move is mechanically
enforceable ("does an active read of `findings/` include archive/? no,
because it's a different path") without requiring every reader to parse
frontmatter correctly; a status field is easy to add but easy to silently
ignore, which is exactly the failure mode this ADR exists to close. Not
ruled out as a *future* refinement layered on top of the directory
convention — just not the primary mechanism.

**Option C — No hard precedence rule; rely on dates/context to disambiguate
staleness at read time.** Rejected — this is what was already in place and
is what produced the live failure (ADR-048's own forward-looking prose was
also stale, and a dated findings doc reads as authoritative on its own
terms). A structural rule (D1) that findings never outrank ADR/PDR removes
the judgment call entirely rather than asking each reader to re-derive it.

#### Consequences

- **Pre-state verification (ADR-018) gets a categorical exclusion.**
  `findings/` — including `findings/archive/` — is never cited as the
  evidence for a "the BC currently does X" claim. This should be reflected
  in the lead-architect role template's admissible-evidence list as a
  follow-up (not this ADR's mechanism — role-template changes ride the
  templates BC's own dispatch path).
- **The archival sweep (D4) is a one-time application of already-decided
  criteria**, not an ongoing commitment to re-triage `findings/` on a
  cadence; future spikes are archived at graduation/abandonment time by
  whoever closes that spike's initiative, per D2.
- **Cross-link to `lead-x7bp`** (progressive-disclosure epic): the
  precedence/disclosure of a *findings* kind specifically is a narrow
  instance of the broader progressive-disclosure question that epic owns.
  This ADR resolves the narrow instance; it does not resolve or shape the
  epic.
