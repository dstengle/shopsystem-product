## Source (pre-modernization)

#### Context

The agent-vault credential spike hit a wall an autonomous agent **cannot**
cross: a human must once read the real `accessToken`/`refreshToken` from a
logged-in `~/.claude/.credentials.json` and store them in the broker. The agent
did the right thing — used a dummy, proved everything creds-free, and recorded
the wall — rather than faking it. That behavior is the template; this ADR
codifies it so any autonomous spike facing a human-gated step handles it the
same way instead of either faking the secret or treating the wall as a verdict
blocker.

##### Pre-state empirical findings (against the artifact/contract surface, ADR-018)

No BC code read or run; no `repos/` on this host. Established from
`findings/agent-vault-credential-spike.md` (lead-jkwo) and the synthesis:

- The agent-vault spike's assertion **(d) passed with a creds-free 401
  kill-line** — a real `request_id` plus an expected failure that a real secret
  would turn into success. This is exactly "prove the plumbing, stop at the
  secret": the plumbing is demonstrated, the secret is not faked.
- The spike reached a **confirm** verdict *with* the human wall recorded — the
  wall did not block the verdict, because the creds-free plumbing was proven.
- The spike **corrected two predicted human walls** (owner registration and CA
  export turned out CLI-doable, not dashboard-only) — i.e. predicted walls must
  be re-tested against reality during execution, not assumed.

#### Decision

An autonomous spike that encounters a human-in-the-loop wall follows this
five-step protocol:

##### D1 — Detect the wall

Identify any step requiring a one-time human secret, an interactive OAuth paste,
a dashboard-only action, or any input the autonomous agent has **no admissible
way** to supply.

##### D2 — Do not fake it; prove everything up to the wall

Substitute a dummy/placeholder and prove *everything up to the wall*. The
creds-free kill-line is the template: produce a **real** artifact (e.g. a real
`request_id`) plus an **expected failure** that a real secret would turn into
success (e.g. a creds-free 401). Faking the secret to manufacture a green result
is forbidden — it would produce a false verdict.

##### D3 — Record the wall explicitly

Record the wall in the findings doc as "the one hard human-gated step," naming
**exactly** what the human must do and **what resumes afterward** (e.g. the
broker auto-refreshes thereafter). The wall is a documented, named artifact, not
an implicit gap.

##### D4 — A spike may reach `confirm` with a recorded wall

The wall becomes a **Phase-2 operational step** — a runbook line, or a scenario
step the operator performs once — **not** a blocker on the spike's verdict. A
spike may reach **confirm** with a recorded human wall **provided the creds-free
plumbing is proven** (D2). agent-vault did exactly this.

##### D5 — Correct predicted walls against reality

Predicted walls must be **re-tested during execution**, not assumed. A step
predicted to need a human may turn out CLI-doable (as two agent-vault walls
did); the findings doc records the corrected reality, not the prediction.

#### Alternatives considered

**Option A — Treat any human-gated step as a hard verdict blocker
(`inconclusive`).** Rejected. agent-vault proved a wall does not prevent a sound
`confirm` when the plumbing up to it is demonstrated creds-free. Blocking on
every wall would have killed a genuinely confirmed spike and discarded a correct
finding.

**Option B — Let the agent fake the secret with a plausible dummy that returns
green.** Rejected, and dangerous. A faked green is a **false verdict** — it
would graduate behavior that was never actually proven. D2's kill-line shape
(real artifact + expected creds-free failure) is the safe equivalent: it proves
the plumbing without manufacturing a false success.

**Option C — Defer the whole spike until a human is available to supply the
secret inline.** Rejected. It stalls autonomous experimentation on operator
availability and discards the value of proving everything else creds-free first.
D4 instead defers only the *wall itself* to a Phase-2 operational step, while the
spike completes and graduates on the proven plumbing.

#### Consequences

- **Autonomous spikes are not blocked by human walls** — they complete, reach a
  sound verdict, and hand the wall forward as an operational step. This is what
  makes overnight autonomous experimentation viable.
- **The wall is graduated, not lost:** the Phase-2 graduation (ADR-029 D4) must
  carry the recorded wall into a runbook line or an operator-performed scenario
  step. PO authoring of the graduation scenarios should pin the operator step
  where one exists.
- **No false confirms:** the do-not-fake rule (D2) keeps the verdict vocabulary
  (ADR-029 D2) honest — a `confirm` always means the plumbing was actually
  proven, with the secret as the single named, deferred gap.
- **Predicted-wall correction (D5)** keeps findings docs accurate to reality, so
  Phase-2 operational steps do not encode walls that turned out not to exist.
