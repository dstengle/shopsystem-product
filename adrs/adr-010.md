## Context

ADR-009 canonized vehicle (b): a BC `clarify` is resolved by dispatching a fresh
outbound message on a new lead-side bead carrying the resolving scenario set or
maintenance instruction. The original dispatch remains in flight; the new bead
is additive. ADR-009 does not specify what the BC's `work_done.scenario_hashes`
field should contain when the work_done is emitted against the new (resolving)
bead's work_id. The omission produced an observable reconciliation failure on
the first BC work_done landing against the canonized pattern.

**Empirical record (2026-05-27):**

- `lead-km9` (2026-05-22) dispatched 5 adoption-doc scenarios to
  `shopsystem-docs`: `7ac28115fe1a2a33`, `b0fe0666bcaa2398`,
  `6055501e8a23a652`, `8c55526c7a83bcd0`, `3874a7f3c34e4f60`.
- The BC's `clarify` (per lead-km9) flagged `b0fe0666bcaa2398` as not
  establishable in its pytest-bdd harness; said nothing about the other four.
- `lead-b34` (clarify-resolution per ADR-009 vehicle (b)) dispatched a single
  replacement scenario (`56babfaeec4514a3`) on a fresh lead bead, framed as
  additive to lead-km9.
- BC's `work_done` on `lead-b34` (2026-05-27) echoed all five hashes in
  `scenario_hashes:`: `56babfaeec4514a3` plus the four unchanged originals. The
  summary text said "Echoing new hash 56babfaeec4514a3 plus the four unchanged
  hashes from lead-km9".
- **Observed BC state:** `repos/shopsystem-docs/features/` contains exactly one
  file, pinning `56babfaeec4514a3` only. The four echoed originals are not in the
  BC's features tree at all. `lead-km9` has no recorded `work_done`.

The BC reasonably (but incorrectly) interpreted "echo all unchanged hashes from
the original dispatch" as the contract for a clarify-resolution work_done. Two
interpretations were available; both are defensible against ADR-009's text:

1. **Strict-subset reading:** `scenario_hashes` lists what the BC has actually
   pinned in `features/` and discharged by this work_done. Sibling hashes from
   the original dispatch that the BC has not yet pinned must NOT be echoed; they
   remain in flight against the original dispatch's work_id.
2. **Full-original-set reading:** since the resolving dispatch is additive to the
   original, the work_done can echo the full original hash set the BC believes
   itself to be discharging across both dispatches, treating one work_done as
   terminating both work_ids.

The BC followed interpretation (2). The interpretation produces a mismatch
between the wire-form claim and the on-disk reality: the work_done asserts
coverage of scenarios that have no corresponding `@scenario_hash` tag in any
feature file in the BC.

**Alternatives considered.**

- **Option A — Adopt the full-original-set reading.** Rejected. The work_done's
  `scenario_hashes` field's value-add is reconciliation; if it can echo hashes
  the BC has not actually pinned, the reconciliation step collapses to "trust the
  BC's text" and the lead Architect's grep-against-`features/` check becomes
  redundant theatre. The strict-subset rule preserves the field's verification
  utility.
- **Option B — Amend ADR-009 in-place with a v2 section.** Rejected for this
  turn because the scope rule is conceptually layered on top of ADR-009 (vehicle
  selection) rather than altering it: ADR-009 still governs which message type
  the resolving dispatch uses; ADR-010 governs what the resolving work_done
  claims. Keeping them as separate documents makes the dependency explicit
  (ADR-010 "Amends" ADR-009) and avoids mutating an accepted ADR's body. If a
  future synthesis ADR consolidates the clarify-resolution rule set, both 009 and
  010 can be superseded together.
- **Option C — Add a `superseded_hashes` companion field to work_done.**
  Rejected as out of scope. Encoding supersession in the message catalog is a
  coordinated schema change; the lead's bead notes already carry the supersession
  record adequately for the current scale. Revisit if reconciliation friction
  becomes routine.

## Decision

**Strict-subset is the canonical rule.** Specifically:

1. A `work_done.scenario_hashes` list MUST be exactly the set of hashes the BC
   has pinned in `features/` AND discharged (BDD passes, reviewer-attested) as
   part of completing the dispatch whose work_id the work_done addresses.
2. For a clarify-resolution work_done specifically: `scenario_hashes` MUST be
   limited to hashes carried in the resolving dispatch's payload. A
   clarify-resolution work_done does NOT extend coverage to sibling hashes from
   the original dispatch — even when the resolving dispatch is framed as additive
   to the original.
3. The original dispatch's work_id remains separately open until its own
   work_done lands, covering its own hashes (minus any that were superseded by
   the clarify-resolution). A "superseded" hash is one the clarify-resolution
   dispatch explicitly replaces (e.g., the replacement scenario
   `56babfaeec4514a3` supersedes the original `b0fe0666bcaa2398`). The lead's
   bead notes are the authoritative record of supersession.
4. **Observable evidence requirement (BC-side):** before emitting a work_done,
   the BC MUST verify each hash in `scenario_hashes` is present in its
   `features/` tree under an `@scenario_hash:<hash>` tag. The verification is a
   discrete pre-emit step. The Reviewer template carried by
   `shopsystem-templates` is the natural place to encode the check; an ADR-010
   follow-up bead tracks that template edit (filed separately, not dispatched
   this turn).
5. **Reconciliation-side (lead Architect):** when a work_done lands, the
   reconciliation step MUST grep `features/` in the BC repo for each echoed hash.
   A hash present in `scenario_hashes` but absent from `features/` is a protocol
   violation — file a follow-up lead-side bead, do not silently close the
   original dispatch.

## Consequences

- The BC Reviewer template carried by `shopsystem-templates` should learn the
  pre-emit hash-presence check (grep `features/` for each hash in
  `work_done.scenario_hashes` before emit; abort if any hash is absent).
  Follow-up bead tracks the template edit; not dispatched this turn (per the
  user's standing scope guidance on this turn).
- The lead Architect's reconciliation procedure tightens: every work_done
  reception runs the grep cross-check on the BC's `features/` tree before closing
  the originating bead. A mismatch produces a follow-up bead (as `lead-apk` was
  filed against the `lead-b34` mismatch).
- `lead-km9` (the original dispatch) remains open. Its eventual closure path:
  either (a) its own work_done lands covering its remaining four hashes — now
  superseded by `lead-eqx`'s wire-form hashes due to canonical-body drift, so
  this path is unlikely; or (b) a future reconciliation bead closes it as
  superseded by `lead-eqx` once `lead-eqx`'s work_done lands and reconciles
  cleanly. Path (b) is the expected route.
- `lead-b34`'s already-landed work_done is grandfathered. ADR-010 applies
  prospectively; the past work_done is the canonized pattern's birth pain, not a
  violation under the rule as it existed at the time.
- The lead-shop role template (`lead-architect.md`) inherits a sharper
  reconciliation procedure: grep-each-hash against BC `features/` on every
  work_done reception. The lead-architect inline template at
  `.claude/agents/lead-architect.md` already requires the `@scenario_hash`
  enumeration step on dispatch (the "Have you enumerated the BC's pinned
  @scenario_hash set" check) — ADR-010 generalizes the same enumeration
  discipline to the reconciliation side. A follow-up bead can edit the canonical
  template in `shopsystem-templates` to make the reconciliation grep explicit;
  not dispatched this turn.
