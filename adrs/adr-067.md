## Context

The product authority ruled on a decision-document coherence model this session,
after reviewing PDR-032 as a motivating specimen. PDR-032 is a single `accepted`
document that a reader must actively *subtract from* to use correctly: it carries
(a) an appetite-exclusion line ("Explicitly OUT of appetite: legacy corpus
migration") that PDR-034 has since made false, (b) a `Consequences`/`Source`
posture describing the hand-authored templates+schema *mechanism* that ADR-059
later "refined" out from above, (c) a "partially amends PDR-031" relationship
with a matching PDR-031 "Amended-by note", and (d) a duplicated
`Source (pre-modernization)` body. To read PDR-032 as current truth a reader must
perform at least four separate acts of in-document discrimination — subtract the
appetite line, subtract the superseded Consequences posture, cross-reference
which half of PDR-031 still survives, and ignore the duplicated source body — all
inside one document whose `status` field says, flatly, `accepted`.

Two structural defects produced that tangle, and the product authority named a
rule for each.

**The amendment/partial-supersede model has no honest status.** ADR-059 had to
reach *up* the lane and "refine" PDR-032's Consequences because part of PDR-032
became false while the rest stayed live. But a document that is partly-true and
partly-superseded has no honest value for its `status` field: `accepted`
overclaims (part of it is dead), and `superseded` underclaims (most of it still
governs). Every partial relationship — `partially-amends`, `refined-by`,
"supersedes only section X" — forces the `status` field to lie, and forces every
reader to re-derive by hand which part survives. The product authority's ruling,
verbatim in intent: "there should not be any amendments. There should be full
supersedes with new documents that copy the existing content with amendments. The
first entry in the changelog could be copying the original document... The idea of
replacing is nothing new and is standard practice in IETF RFCs" (the Obsoletes
model).

**Lane-crossing is a primary generator of amendment tangles.** PDR-032's
`Consequences` reached down into the *architecture* lane — it described the
hand-authored templates+schema *mechanism*, a "how" — which is exactly the hook
ADR-059 then had to reach back up and grab in order to "refine" it. Had PDR-032
confined itself to the product lane (ownership: which BC owns the type system),
there would have been nothing in it for an ADR to refine: the mechanism change
would have lived entirely inside ADR-space. The product authority named this
too: "combining PDRs and an ADR seems like crossing lanes."

This ADR is a sibling of the coherence-discipline family already established:
ADR-065 (spike/findings material is never authoritative over ADR/PDR) fixed the
*authority precedence* of one document class; this ADR fixes the *self-containment
and lane discipline* of the decision-document class. Both rest on ADR-018's
contract/artifact-surface reasoning basis, and both are enforced (prospectively)
by the coherence gate whose mechanism ADR-059 owns under PDR-032's charter.

**Alternatives considered.**

*Option A — Keep the amendment/partial-supersede model; rely on careful reading.*
Rejected. This is the status quo that produced the PDR-031↔PDR-032↔ADR-059 tangle
above. It requires every reader to re-derive which portion of an `accepted` doc is
still live, and it forces the `status` field to carry a value that is true of only
part of the document. Careful reading is a probabilistic gate, not a structural
one — the same class of reasoning ADR-059 itself rejected for template↔schema
drift.

*Option B — Allow partial supersession but track it with rich typed edges
(`refined-by`, `partially-amends`, section anchors).* Rejected. Richer edges make
the graph more expressive but do not fix the core defect: a partially-superseded
document still has no honest `status`, and a consolidation still requires chasing
and rewriting every inbound edge that pointed at the retired portion. Expressiveness
here buys precision about a relationship that should not exist.

*Option C — Whole-document, full-supersede copy-forward (the IETF Obsoletes model),
plus strict lane discipline (chosen).* Every change to any part of a decision doc
is a NEW whole document that copies surviving content forward and FULLY supersedes
its predecessor; each doc stays in one lane; cross-lane relationships are references,
never amendments. `status` is always honest (a doc is wholly `accepted` or wholly
`superseded`); a superseded number persists as a navigable tombstone so copy-forward
costs nothing at the citation layer. This is standard IETF RFC practice and
generalizes cleanly to the coherence gate.

## Decision

**D1 — Decision documents are self-contained; no amendments, full-supersede
copy-forward.** A decision document states the *complete current truth* for its
subject. You may not partially-amend, "refine", or "supersede only part of" a
prior decision document. To change ANY part of a decision doc, issue a NEW
document that:

1. **copies the surviving content forward** into the new document (so the new
   document alone is the complete current truth — a reader never has to consult
   the predecessor to reconstruct what still holds);
2. **FULLY supersedes** the predecessor: the predecessor's `status` flips to
   `superseded`, and a matching `superseded-by` back-edge is recorded on it
   pointing at the successor;
3. records, as the **first changelog entry** of the new document, that it copies
   the predecessor forward (naming the predecessor id) — so the copy-forward
   provenance is explicit rather than silent.

This is the IETF RFC "Obsoletes" model. The decisive property it protects is that
**the `status` field must never lie.** A "partially superseded" document has no
honest status — `accepted` overclaims and `superseded` underclaims — which is
precisely why partial relationships are banned. Whole-document supersession keeps
`status` categorically true and removes the reader's re-derivation burden.

**D2 — Keep decisions in their lane.** Each decision-document class carries only
its own lane's content:

- **PDR = product / what / who** — ownership, taxonomy, scope, appetite,
  product-vocabulary commitments.
- **ADR = architecture / how** — mechanism, implementation, structural
  contracts.

A decision document MUST NOT contain the other lane's content. When PDR-032's
`Consequences` described the templates+schema *mechanism* (a "how") it crossed
into the ADR lane, and that crossing is exactly what obliged ADR-059 to reach up
and "refine" a PDR — the amendment tangle D1 forbids. Cross-lane relationships are
expressed as **references** (`derives-from`, cross-ref, `incorporates`), never as
amendments or supersessions. An ADR derives-from the PDR whose charter it
implements; it does not edit that PDR. Lane-crossing is a primary source of
amendment tangles, standing right beside non-atomic bundling.

**D3 — Scope: this governs DECISION DOCS; scenarios are explicitly excluded.**

- **In scope: ADR and PDR** — the two decision-document classes above.
- **In scope: `brief`** — a `brief` is a numbered, frontmatter-typed
  commitment/spec artifact cited *by number* (e.g. `brief-019`, `brief-022`), and
  it carries the same `status`/supersession frontmatter machinery as PDR/ADR.
  Every property D1 protects applies to it verbatim: a superseded brief number
  persists as a navigable tombstone (copy-forward is free at the citation layer),
  and its `status` field faces the identical "must never lie" honesty constraint.
  Its lane is the product/what side (a brief shapes *what*), so D2 applies too: a
  brief must not carry architecture-lane mechanism content. Brief is therefore
  **IN** scope. (Reasoned position recorded rather than assumed, per the routing
  ask: the discriminator is the citation model, and brief is cited by number, so
  it lands on the same side of the line as PDR/ADR.)
- **Explicitly OUT of scope: scenarios.** Scenarios are cited by a **stable
  `@scenario_hash` identity** across the whole system and its parity/retirement
  gates — not by number. Copy-forward-renumbering a scenario would break every
  citation that binds to its identity tag, so scenarios deliberately keep the
  stable-identity amendment model (a stable identity tag plus an evolving content
  digest; see the retirement convention in ADR-064). D1's copy-forward and D3's
  by-number tombstone are *not in tension* with the scenario model — each rule is
  correct for how its artifact class is referenced. Decision docs are cited by
  number (a retired number is a free tombstone); scenarios are cited by identity
  hash (a renumber is a break). The rule follows the citation model, not the file
  format.

**D4 — Directive to the coherence gate (follow-on, NOT dispatched here).** The
coherence gate (mechanism owned by ADR-059 under PDR-032's charter) should, as a
named follow-on:

1. **REJECT partial-amendment relationships** for decision docs — a
   `partially-amends`, `refined-by`, or "supersedes only section X" edge between
   decision documents is a coherence finding, not a valid edge.
2. **Require supersession to be whole-document** — a `supersedes`/`superseded-by`
   edge must be matched by the back-edge on the predecessor and by the
   predecessor's `status: superseded`; a one-sided or partial supersession is a
   finding.
3. **Resolve `superseded-by` TRANSITIVELY** — an inbound edge that points at a
   superseded document auto-follows to its successor, so a consolidation does not
   require chasing and rewriting every inbound edge by hand.

D4 is recorded here as a directive/consequence only; it is NOT dispatched by this
ADR. Its enforcement is a named follow-on to shopsystem-knowledge (see
Consequences).

## Consequences

- **`status` is always honest, and reading a decision doc is a single act.** A
  reader of any in-scope document reads exactly one document and gets the complete
  current truth; there is no "subtract the stale line, cross-reference which half
  survives" burden of the PDR-032 kind. The `status` field is categorically true.

- **Existing partial relationships are GRANDFATHERED until superseded via
  copy-forward.** Documents that today carry amendment / refined-by / partial
  relationships — notably `pdr-031`↔`pdr-032` (partial-amends + Amended-by note)
  and `adr-059`→`pdr-032` (Consequences "refined") — are NOT retroactively
  rewritten by this ADR. They are grandfathered and become compliant the next time
  their subject is touched, at which point the touch is a full copy-forward
  supersession rather than another amendment.

- **The FIRST application of this rule is the knowledge-BC governance
  consolidation.** A new PDR that copies `pdr-031` + `pdr-032`'s *product* content
  forward and FULLY supersedes both (flipping both to `superseded` with matching
  back-edges) is the first exercise of D1/D2. That consolidation is a SEPARATE
  drafting task — it is not performed by this ADR.

- **Gate enforcement (D4) is a named follow-on, not dispatched here.** A bead is
  to be filed against shopsystem-knowledge for the coherence gate to enforce
  no-partial-amendment (D4.1), whole-document supersession with matching back-edge
  (D4.2), and transitive `superseded-by` resolution (D4.3). This ADR does not
  dispatch it.

- **Scenarios are untouched.** The scenario stable-identity amendment model and its
  retirement convention (ADR-064) are explicitly out of scope and unchanged; D3
  records *why* the two models coexist correctly rather than putting them in
  tension.

- **Draft status.** This ADR is `proposed`. The product authority is offline and
  ratifies on return; per the "withhold the supersede edge while proposed" pattern
  (PDR-034 lines 137–144), no other document's `status`, `superseded-by`, or
  amendment relationship is touched, flipped, or superseded by this draft. The
  grandfathered relationships above remain exactly as they are until a
  copy-forward supersession is separately authored and ratified.
