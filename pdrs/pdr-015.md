## Source (pre-modernization)

#### 1. Why a PDR (and not brief + scenarios alone)

The brief commits intent and the scenarios pin behavior. A PDR is warranted
here because the "completed = pinned & demonstrated" definition and the
"unify incremental + on-demand only if lightweight" trade-off are decisions
whose **"why" will be re-asked** the first time an Architect or Implementer
hits the path-unification fork. Per the PO discipline, a PDR is written for a
scenario *family* whose rationale is non-obvious — this qualifies. It does
**not** pre-empt the Architect's structural calls; it bounds them.

---

#### 2. HMW (from brief 009)

> **How might we** make per-scenario completion a durable, queryable fact —
> an authoritative append-only journal in each BC, mirrored as a lead-side
> snapshot — so that *"is this scenario done?"* and *"what is outstanding
> system-wide?"* are **lookups**, not manual reconstructions — **without**
> prematurely fixing which BC owns the journal, the snapshot store, or the
> journal-pull vehicle?

The HMW is deliberately broad enough to admit more than one mechanism shape
and is agnostic about owners. Identity throughout is the **block-only
canonical hash** (ADR-019, scenario-117) — that is settled upstream and is
**not** a branch here.

---

#### 3. Opportunity-solution tree (branches the HMW admits)

**Outcome (root):** "is scenario X done?" and "what is outstanding?" are
constant-time lookups, not per-`work_done` sweeps.

**Opportunity A — atomic per-scenario completion lookup** *(the seed; MVP
element #1).* Keyed on block-only canonical hash → definite yes/no.
- Solution branches (Architect's call): the completion set is read from the
  lead snapshot; OR resolved live against BC journals; OR a unified read
  surface. The MVP scenarios (01/02) pin the *answer*, not the *read path*.

**Opportunity B — authoritative durable record of completion.**
- **Branch B1 — BC-authoritative append-only journal** (decision #3). Each
  BC owns the truth of what it has completed; the lead mirrors it. Chosen
  *posture* (the stakeholder fixed this); the *store* and *owning BC*
  (`shopsystem-scenarios` per ADR-019? `shopsystem-messaging` as transport?
  `shopsystem-templates` as tag tooling?) are open.
- **Branch B2 (rejected by decision #3) — lead-authoritative ledger.** The
  lead's record as source of truth. Rejected: secondary job (a),
  agent-failure recovery, requires the BC to be authoritative so the lead
  can rebuild from BC journals. Recorded as rejected so it is not re-litigated.

**Opportunity C — lead-side materialized system view.**
- **Branch C1 — incremental mirror** from the `work_done` the lead already
  receives (scenario 04). Cheap, continuous, but only as complete as the
  stream it saw.
- **Branch C2 — full on-demand reconcile** by pulling BC journals (scenario
  05). Complete and recovery-capable, but heavier per invocation.
- **Branch C3 — lightweight unification of C1 + C2** (decision #3's
  *preference*). Prefer ONLY if it does not add heavy complexity / resource
  cost; otherwise keep C1 and C2 as separate paths. **This is the central
  fork the Architect resolves** (see §4).

**Opportunity D — correct system-wide denominator.**
- **Branch D1 (chosen by decision #2)** — denominator is ALL canonical
  scenarios under this repo's `features/`, including never-dispatched ones
  (scenario 06). The enumeration source is `features/` on disk (the
  `scenarios` tooling already walks it); whether outstanding is computed in
  the lead or a BC is open.
- **Branch D2 (rejected) — dispatched-only denominator.** Rejected: it hides
  the authored-but-never-built class the seed story is a symptom of.

**D1 sub-decision (resolved 2026-06-08, stakeholder dave) — orphan
completions are flagged, not counted.** A boundary of D1: *what counts when a
BC journals a completed hash with no match in canonical `features/`?*
**Resolved (option A): canonical `features/` is the sole authority.** Such an
orphan completion is **flagged as an unrecognized/orphan anomaly** and
surfaced for investigation — **excluded from the coverage count AND from the
outstanding denominator** (scenario **07**). It is never silently dropped and
never made first-class. **Rationale (do not relitigate):** canonical hashes
are content-addressed and **retire-and-replace** — editing a body mints a new
hash and the author rewrites the `@scenario_hash:` tag (scenario **117-E**
integrity / edit-detection; **117-D** Feature-line-edit invariance). An
absent-from-canonical completed hash is therefore not a legitimate steady
state; it can only arise from **(i)** the in-flight wire-`scenarios[].hash`
vs. disk-`@scenario_hash:`-tag divergence defect (**lead-ji28** in_progress;
**lead-gw60** open — defect-(a) fix unverified), **(ii)** transient
propagation / version-lag (BC completed a now-retired hash), or **(iii)** a
BC-local / never-promoted scenario. Flagging doubles as a **free detector for
the lead-ji28 divergence class**. Caveat: until **lead-gw60** closes a flagged
orphan may *be* that known defect — the desired surfacing, not a
contradiction. This resolves the sub-decision *within* D1; it does not reopen
the chosen denominator.

**Deliberately NOT on the tree (out of MVP scope, decision #4):** "currently
green" / drift-tracking; agent-failure recovery *workflow* (the journal is
made authoritative *for* it, but the workflow is later); scenario-level
in-flight progress; roll-up into higher-level units. These motivate B1's
posture and C2's existence but are not authored as scenarios.

---

#### 4. The one trade-off the stakeholder pre-weighted

**Question:** are the incremental-reflect path (C1) and the
full-on-demand-reconcile path (C2) **unified** into one mechanism, or kept
**separate**?

**Stakeholder steer (decision #3):** *prefer a lightweight unification ONLY
if it does not add heavy complexity / resource cost; otherwise keep them
separate.* Weigh incremental-work-amount against
full-on-demand-reconcile capability.

**What this PDR commits:** the **behavioral surface** of both paths —
incremental reflect (scenario 04) and on-demand reconcile (scenario 05) —
must exist. Whether they share one code path (C3) is the Architect's
structural call, made after empirical pre-state. The Architect should treat
C3 as the *preferred default to test*, and fall back to separate C1/C2 if
unification is not lightweight. Recording this here means the fork is decided
against a known steer, not re-derived.

---

#### 5. What this PDR does NOT decide (Architect's, post pre-state)

- **Which BC owns the journal, the snapshot store, and outstanding
  computation.** Probable surfaces: `shopsystem-scenarios` (block-only hash
  + `features/` enumeration, ADR-019), `shopsystem-messaging` (the
  `work_done` transport and a possible journal-pull message type),
  `shopsystem-templates` (`@scenario_hash:` tag tooling). Decomposition is
  the Architect's call.
- **The on-demand journal-pull vehicle** — new `shop-msg` message type,
  extension of an existing one, or a CLI surface on an owning BC.
- **C1/C2 path unification (§4)** — preferred-but-conditional; Architect
  resolves.
- **The message-type discriminator** per dispatch (`assign_scenarios` /
  `request_bugfix` / `request_maintenance`), applied after pre-state.

---

#### 6. Consequences

- The Architect inherits a bounded option space and a pre-weighted trade-off;
  framing is not re-opened at dispatch.
- "Completed = pinned & demonstrated" and the BC-authoritative posture are
  recorded with rationale, so they are not re-litigated when recovery
  (secondary job a) is eventually authored — that future brief builds on
  branch C2.
- Identity is the block-only canonical hash everywhere (ADR-019 /
  scenario-117); no branch reintroduces title/bead/dispatch-record keying.
