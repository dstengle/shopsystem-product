## Source (pre-modernization)

#### Point of intent

"Bootstrap makes it releasable" is the standing strategic bet: an adopter runs
the bootstrap, gets a green lead shop, and then the lead drives the product into
existence. Today a freshly bootstrapped lead lands at a prompt with a scaffold
and **no product** — and, per the PRIME DIRECTIVE, walks its idle-detection
checklist, finds no beads / no mailbox traffic, and idles. That is the wrong
posture for an empty repo: there is enormous work to do (shape the product), but
none of it is yet *recorded* as a bead or scenario for the checklist to find. The
adopter is left to volunteer "okay, what now?" — exactly the friction the
framework-as-product fork exists to remove.

#### Decision

Add a new router trigger to the canonical lead primer (`shop-templates`,
rendered into every product's lead), in two parts:

1. **Detection signal (scenario 223).** Define "effectively empty /
   no-product-defined" observably: the absence of any *product-bearing* artifact
   — substantive brief, PDR, `features/` scenario, or product-bearing bead —
   beyond what the bootstrap scaffold itself writes (canonical-managed files,
   placeholder shop primer, initialized-but-empty beads registry).

2. **Proactive initiation (scenario 224).** On detecting that state at session
   start or during the idle-detection checklist, the router opens a
   product-discovery conversation with the product authority **instead of
   declaring idle**, seeding from the discovery skill furniture (JTBD,
   problem-framing-canvas, opportunity-solution-tree) and capturing the result as
   the seed brief / intent.

#### Design alignment — fires the existing gate, does not duplicate it

The lead primer already carries the **product-authority discovery gate**, which
establishes WHERE discovery dialogue happens: at the main-agent / router level,
because discovery subagents are non-interactive and cannot conduct it. This PDR
does **not** restate that placement rule; it adds the **trigger** that fires it
proactively. The empty-repo trigger leads the router into the gate's dialogue —
it must not pin a non-interactive subagent doing the talking.

Relationship to the idle-detection checklist: the empty-repo state is a *new
positive item* that pre-empts idle. Where the checklist's five items search for
recorded work, this trigger recognizes the absence of any product at all as
itself a ready action — shaping the product — that outranks idling.

#### What this leaves open (scope thinness — product round-trip)

- **Exact membership of the "product-bearing artifact" set.** Whether an
  initialized-but-empty beads registry, a placeholder shop primer, or
  bootstrap-authored housekeeping beads count as scaffold vs. product is the
  PO's provisional reading. Flagged for confirmation when the authority returns.
- **One-shot vs. resumable.** Whether the trigger fires only on the very first
  empty session, or re-fires every session until a product exists, is unpinned;
  the scenarios as written key on the *state*, not on session ordinality, which
  implies re-fire-until-shaped. Confirm with authority.
- **Which discovery skill seeds by default.** The scenarios name the furniture as
  a set ("such as ...") rather than mandating one entry point. Left to the
  router's judgment at conversation time.
