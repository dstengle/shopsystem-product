## Context

The operator's request arrived **solution-shaped and converged**: "the
Implementer breaks work down poorly → make it break work down better." Run
through the problem-framing-canvas, it opens up before any intent is committed.

- **Symptom:** 6k-line implementation files; work broken down poorly *inside*
  the BC.
- **Why unsolved:** unclear + deprioritized. §4.1/§4.2 already name an
  Implementer activity, "Break down into sub-issues" — so the activity exists.
  What is missing is a *discipline criterion* on what a good breakdown is, and a
  role whose posture is design-quality rather than make-it-pass.
- **Two assumptions surfaced and challenged.** *Assumption A* — "the breakdown
  is missing" — is **false**: §4.2 lists the activity, so this is a discipline
  gap in an existing activity, not a missing activity (this reframes the whole
  PDR — no activity is being added). *Assumption B* — "the Implementer should
  obviously own design quality" — is **premature convergence**: the
  Implementer's posture is make-it-pass (§4.1), and design quality may be a
  different role's posture (a BC-local architect). Who owns the discipline is an
  architecture decision (lead-cnbu), not a PO call, and must not be baked into
  the immediate bet.
- **Who experiences it.** BC-shop agents — the Implementer builds a 6k-line
  file without a right-sizing criterion, and the Reviewer must adversarially
  probe a monolith, which is slower and leakier. The operator / system-architect
  periodically reads BC code and finds it hard to review and evolve — the voice
  that filed the observation. The periodic reader (future maintainer, human or
  agent) faces a monolithic unit that hides which behavior lives where, making
  evolution high-risk. Left out of the framing was a **BC-local architect
  role** — design sensibility up front, distinct from make-it-pass — which the
  operator named (lead-ir9m decision 2) but which is not in §4.1's two-role
  catalogue.
- **Pains, ranked (JTBD, framework-as-product fork).** ACUTE and operator-cited:
  6k-line files hold many behaviors; units are layer- or task-shaped, not
  behavior-shaped. Acute (derived): adversarial review must probe a monolith;
  evolution is high-risk with behaviors tangled and no single-responsibility
  boundary. Mild: nobody owns "is this the right size?" — an ownerless seam that
  is build-trap-adjacent, since the build is free and big units ship without a
  focus gate. The functional primary job is "turn an assigned scenario set into
  BC code I can review, trust, and evolve one behavior at a time"; the secondary
  "locate where a single behavior lives" is marked **inference**, not
  load-bearing for the immediate bet.
- **Spec grounding.** §4.1's silent-inference clause states a capable agent
  "will fill gaps from BC code or world knowledge unless its role is equipped
  with explicit criteria," and that such criteria live in the template/skill,
  iterable without spec churn — which is exactly the shape of the immediate bet.
  §4.4 keeps product intent at the lead (Reviewer-proposes-tightening → lead PO
  decides; no BC-local PO, lead-ir9m decision 1), but a BC-local *architect*
  (structural/design, not product intent) is a separate, architect-owned
  question. PDR-001 explicitly left open whether BC-shop templates have the same
  partial §4.2 coverage; this PDR answers one slice and flags the rest.

## Options considered

Formal PDR-house options:

- **(A) Add a new "right-size the breakdown" activity to §4.2.** Rejected — the
  breakdown activity already exists; this is a discipline gap, and §4.1 says
  such criteria live in the template, iterable without spec churn.
- **(B) Bake the owner into the immediate bet (Implementer owns design
  quality).** Rejected — premature convergence (Assumption B); the role-split is
  the Architect's decision (lead-cnbu). Pin the discipline, not the role.
- **(B′) Design the BC-local architect role and the ADR hierarchy now.**
  Rejected for this slice — architect-owned, and the operator scoped them as
  named-not-designed (lead-ir9m); designing them here would couple the shippable
  discipline to unresolved structural decisions.
- **(C, chosen)** Record the framing / measurable outcome / OST bets here; ship
  the immediate decomposition discipline as Gherkin against the BC role template
  (role-agnostic, guidance-or-pending markers); name the structural bets as
  architect-owned / guidance pending.

Opportunity-solution-tree divergence weighed underneath: **O1** (no right-sizing
criterion in the breakdown activity) was selected. **O2** (no role holds
design-quality posture up front) and **O3** (no design-decision record at the BC
level) are structural and architect-owned. **O4** (author thinner scenarios at
the lead so the BC can't build big) was rejected — the lead's `work-splitting`
skill already right-sizes *requirements*, but a thin scenario can still be
implemented as a monolith; the pain is internal, so the lever must be internal.
For O1, the chosen solution **S1a** is a right-sizing checklist criterion in the
template; **S1b** (a hard numeric line cap) was rejected as a constraint that
incentivizes horizontal splitting to dodge the cap; **S1c** (Reviewer rejects
oversized units at the gate only) was rejected for pushing cost to the gate
after the monolith is already built, though the Reviewer gate remains the
backstop. **S2** (BC-local architect role) and **S3** (three-tier ADR hierarchy:
framework / system-global / BC-local) are named as structural solutions,
architect-owned, not designed here.

## Decision

1. The BC role template SHALL carry a **decomposition / right-sizing
   discipline** on its existing §4.2 "break down into sub-issues" activity —
   developed as a *discipline of an existing activity*, not a new activity.
2. The discipline SHALL define a good unit as a **thin vertical slice that pins
   one coherent behavior**, and SHALL forbid layer-shaped units ("front-end")
   and task-shaped units ("set up the DB").
3. The discipline SHALL require that a **unit carrying multiple When/Then
   behaviors is split** into focused single-behavior units, and that thin slices
   trace to the assigned scenarios.
4. "Right-sizing" SHALL be **named as a discipline** carrying a sufficiency
   criterion or an explicit **"guidance pending"** marker (the PDR-001 bar).
5. The discipline SHALL stay **agnostic about which BC role owns it**
   (Implementer vs. a future bc-architect) — that role-split is the Architect's
   decision (lead-cnbu). The scenarios pin the discipline, not the role
   structure.
6. The **BC-local architect role** and the **three-tier ADR hierarchy** are
   named as **structural bets, architect-owned, guidance pending** — NOT
   designed in this PDR.
7. This PDR is **intent**; the BC-role-template restructure is templates-BC work
   dispatched via `assign_scenarios` (dispatch HELD while the templates BC is
   offline). The scenarios in `features/templates/154`–`158` pin it.

The immediate bet's **desired outcome is measurable** (the build-trap gate,
PDR-012, requires it): right-size BC work units so a typical assigned unit pins
*one* coherent behavior. The signal is stated as artifact-surface proxies — the
single-responsibility proxy (share of units whose demonstration exercises >1
distinct When/Then moves from "common today" to "rare"), the right-sizing proxy
(each scenario traces to a thin vertical slice, not a layer or a task), and the
secondary review-loop-friction proxy (Reviewer "unit bundles unrelated
behaviors" findings trend to zero). These are **proxies, not instrumented
metrics** — the framework has no file-size telemetry — observable on the
artifact surface (ADR-018), judged rather than measured. The bet's hypothesis:
*if the template carries a right-sizing discipline defining a good unit as a
thin vertical slice pinning one coherent behavior (forbidding layer/task
shapes), the single-responsibility proxy moves from "monoliths common" to
"multi-behavior units rare," because the building agent now has an explicit
criterion instead of filling the §4.1 gap from world knowledge.* The success
signal is recorded *before* dispatch so the returned work is judged against the
outcome, not admired as output, even though the build (a template edit +
scenarios) is nearly free.

#### 1. Problem framing (problem-framing-canvas — three passes)

The operator's request arrives **solution-shaped and converged**: "the
Implementer breaks work down poorly → make it break work down better." The
canvas forces it open before any intent is committed.

##### Pass 1 — Look Inward (what are we assuming?)

- **Symptom:** 6k-line implementation files; work broken down poorly inside the
  BC.
- **Why isn't it solved yet?** *Unclear + deprioritized.* §4.1/§4.2 already
  name an Implementer activity "Break down into sub-issues" — so the activity
  exists. There is no *discipline criterion* on what a good breakdown is, and
  no role whose posture is design-quality rather than make-it-pass.
- **How are WE part of the problem?** Two assumptions surfaced and challenged:
  - *Assumption A (challenged):* "the breakdown is missing." **False** — §4.2
    lists "Break down into sub-issues" as an existing Implementer activity.
    This is a **discipline gap in an existing activity**, not a missing
    activity. (This reframes the whole PDR: we are *not* adding an activity.)
  - *Assumption B (challenged):* "the Implementer should obviously own design
    quality." **Premature convergence.** The Implementer's posture is
    *make-it-pass* (§4.1); design quality may be a *different role's* posture (a
    BC-local architect). **We must not bake the role-owner into the immediate
    bet.** Who owns the discipline is an architecture decision (`lead-cnbu`),
    not a PO call.
- **Premature-convergence check:** the request converges on "Implementer breaks
  down better." We deliberately decline to converge on the *owner*; we converge
  only on *the discipline must exist and be testable*.

##### Pass 2 — Look Outward (who experiences it?)

- **BC-shop agents** — the Implementer building a 6k-line file works without a
  right-sizing criterion; the Reviewer must adversarially probe a monolith,
  which is slower and leakier.
- **The operator / system-architect** — periodically reads BC code and finds it
  hard to review and evolve; this is the voice that filed the observation.
- **The periodic reader of the code** (future maintainer, human or agent) — a
  monolithic unit hides which behavior lives where; evolution is high-risk.
- **Who's been left out of the framing?** A **BC-local architect role** — a
  role whose job is design sensibility *up front*, distinct from the
  Implementer's make-it-pass posture. The operator named this (lead-ir9m
  decision 2) but it is **not** in §4.1's two-role catalogue. Flagging it is a
  scope decision; designing it is the Architect's (`lead-cnbu`), so it is
  **named, not designed, here**.
- **Who benefits from the status quo?** Nobody owns "is this unit the right
  size?" — it is process friction no role currently holds. That ownerless seam
  is exactly the build-trap-adjacent gap: build is free, so big units ship
  without a focus gate.

##### Pass 3 — Reframe

> **Problem statement:** *BC-shop agents and the system-architect struggle to
> review and evolve a BC because the Implementer's existing sub-issue breakdown
> (§4.2) carries no right-sizing discipline — so work lands as oversized,
> layer- or task-shaped units (6k-line files) instead of thin vertical slices
> that each pin one coherent behavior. This leads to slow adversarial review,
> high-risk evolution, and design quality nobody owns — and it was overlooked
> because we assumed the breakdown activity's mere existence implied a
> breakdown discipline, and assumed the make-it-pass Implementer was the
> obvious owner.*

> **How Might We:** *How might we equip the BC role with a decomposition /
> right-sizing discipline so that assigned work is broken into thin vertical
> slices each pinning a coherent behavior — without prematurely fixing which
> BC role owns the discipline?*

The HMW is deliberately broad enough to admit more than one solution (a
checklist criterion in the Implementer's posture; a separate bc-architect role;
a splitting skill the template references) and is **agnostic about the owner**.

#### 3. Opportunity-Solution Tree

##### Desired outcome (measurable — Discipline 2 anchor)

The build-trap gate (PDR-012) requires a **measurable** outcome, not a theme.

> **Outcome:** *Right-size BC work units so a typical assigned unit pins
> **one** coherent behavior.* Stated as an X→Y signal:
>
> - **Single-responsibility proxy:** the share of BC sub-issues / units whose
>   demonstration exercises **>1 distinct When/Then behavior** moves from
>   **"common today" (6k-line monoliths bundle many) → "rare"** (a unit with
>   multiple behaviors is the exception that was deliberately not split).
> - **Right-sizing proxy:** each assigned scenario traces to a **thin vertical
>   slice** (a behavior-shaped unit), not to a layer ("front-end") or a task
>   ("set up the DB").

>   findings attributable to *"the unit bundles unrelated behaviors"* trend
>   toward zero.

accountable to an outcome rather than admired as output.

#### 4. Why this is a discipline gap, not a new activity (spec grounding)

- **§4.1 / §4.2:** the Implementer **already** "breaks the work down into
  sub-issues" — it is an existing activity in the authoritative §4.2 catalogue.
  So this PDR develops the *discipline* of an existing activity; it does **not**
  add an activity. (Same shape as PDR-001 decision #2: name the activity, carry
  a sufficiency criterion or a "guidance pending" marker.)
- **§4.1 silent-inference clause:** §4.1 states a capable agent "will fill gaps
  from BC code or world knowledge unless its role is equipped with explicit
  criteria" — and that **those criteria live in the BC role template / skill,
  not in this spec, so they can be iterated without spec churn.** This PDR's
  immediate bet is *exactly* that: an explicit right-sizing criterion in the
  template, iterable without touching the spec.
- **§4.4 / no BC-PO (principled):** product **intent stays at the lead** —
  §4.4's loop is Reviewer-proposes-tightening → **lead PO decides**. There is
  no BC-local PO because forking product intent into the BC is rejected
  (lead-ir9m decision 1). A BC-local *architect* (structural/design) is a
  different question and is the architect's to decide — design ≠ product intent.
- **PDR-001 open question:** PDR-001 explicitly left open "do BC-shop templates
  have the same partial coverage against §4.2?" This PDR answers one slice of
  it (the breakdown activity's discipline) and flags the rest (bc-architect
  role coverage) to the Architect.

   behaviors is split** into focused single-behavior units, and that thin
   slices **trace to the assigned scenarios**.
4. "Right-sizing" SHALL be **named as a discipline** carrying a sufficiency
   criterion or an explicit **"guidance pending"** marker (PDR-001 decision #2
   bar).
5. The discipline SHALL stay **agnostic about which BC role owns it**
   (Implementer vs. a future bc-architect) — that role-split is the Architect's
   decision (`lead-cnbu`). The scenarios pin the **discipline**, not the role
   structure.
6. The **BC-local architect role** and the **three-tier ADR hierarchy** are
   **named as structural bets, architect-owned, guidance pending** — NOT
   designed in this PDR.
7. This PDR is **intent**; the BC-role-template restructure is templates-BC
   work dispatched via `assign_scenarios` (dispatch HELD while the templates BC
   is offline). The scenarios in `features/templates/154`–`158` pin it.

#### What this leaves open (pending)

- **Which BC role owns the discipline** (Implementer vs. bc-architect) —
  architect-owned (`lead-cnbu`), guidance pending.
- **The BC-local architect role design** — architect-owned, guidance pending.
- **The three-tier ADR hierarchy mechanism**, especially the system-global tier
  (per-product, not template-delivered, needs a new home/convention) —
  architect-owned, guidance pending.
- **Whether the discipline ships as enrichment of the breakdown activity or as
  its own template subsection** — template-authoring decision owned by the
  templates BC; the scenarios pin that the discipline and its criteria appear,
  not the heading structure.
- **An instrumented design-quality metric** (real file-size / coupling
  telemetry) — out of scope; the outcome here is an observable artifact-surface
  proxy (ADR-018), not instrumentation.
