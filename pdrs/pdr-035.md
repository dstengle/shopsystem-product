## Context

`shopsystem-knowledge` is the framework's knowledge bounded context. This PDR is
a **consolidation**: it states the *complete current product truth* about that BC
in one self-contained document, copying forward the surviving product content of
two predecessors and (at ratification — see the supersession-staging note below)
fully superseding both:

- **PDR-031** (`proposed`, never ratified) — founded the BC as a kind-extensible,
  discovery-first knowledge context.
- **PDR-032** (`accepted`, incorporated) — extended that founding so the BC also
  OWNS the artifact type system and the coherence gate.

It is authored under **ADR-067**, which rules that a decision document states the
complete current truth for its subject; that changing any part requires a NEW
whole-document copy-forward that FULLY supersedes the predecessor (the IETF
Obsoletes model, never a partial amendment/refine); and that each decision
document stays in its lane (**PDR = product / what / who**; **ADR = architecture /
how**). This consolidation is the first exercise of that convention (ADR-067
Consequences names it explicitly as a separate drafting task).

**Lane discipline (ADR-067 D2).** This PDR carries **product / what / who content
only**. It states *what* the BC is, *what* it owns, and *who* answers for it. It
deliberately carries **no mechanism/how**: the format-management mechanism
(per-type `typedef` single source → generator → read-only template + JSON Schema
fragment + `--check` drift gate, and the projection-generation determinism /
byte-stability the discovery layer relies on) is **ADR-059's** decision and is
**referenced, not restated here**. The predecessors' lane-crossing — PDR-032's
`Consequences` describing the hand-authored templates+schema *mechanism* — is
exactly what obliged ADR-059 to reach up and "refine" a PDR; this consolidation
does not reproduce that crossing.

**The job-to-be-done (why this BC exists).** *When I am about to author a new
decision (ADR/PDR/brief), help me discover whether the framework has already
decided this, decided the opposite, or decided something this replaces — so I do
not silently create a contradiction or a redundant decision.* The observed
behavior change bought: a decision-author, at authoring time, is shown the
relevant existing decisions and told whether the new decision is already covered,
contradicts one, or supersedes one — before the new decision is committed. Today
that consultation is manual and unreliable, which is how the framework accumulated
contradictory and stale decisions (the fabro FC1–FC4 cases). The JTBD is
**discovery**, not enforcement; enforcement is a narrower, opt-in job.

**Provenance nuance surfaced for ratification (do not decide silently).** The two
predecessors are in different states: **PDR-032 is `accepted` and incorporated**
(current-state.md carries it in `incorporates`), while **PDR-031 is `proposed` and
was never ratified**. So this consolidation both *supersedes an accepted,
incorporated document* (PDR-032) and *subsumes a never-ratified proposed document*
(PDR-031). Under ADR-067's whole-document Obsoletes model the modeling for both is
the same edge — full supersession with a `superseded-by` back-edge and a
`superseded` status — which keeps a navigable tombstone at PDR-031's number rather
than dropping it silently and orphaning any citation of it. **The product
authority should confirm at ratification that whole-doc supersession is the right
modeling for retiring a never-ratified predecessor (vs. simply deleting/dropping
PDR-031)**, per ADR-067. This PDR assumes "supersede-as-tombstone" pending that
confirmation.

## Options considered

The surviving *product-level* options from both predecessors, carried forward:

**On the BC's shape and primary capability (from PDR-031):**

- **Gate-centric, always-on.** The BC's centerpiece is a coherence/invariant gate.
  Rejected as the primary posture: the gate is a narrow enforcement job, not the
  everyday need; the prototype over-invested here and under-built the everyday
  authoring pass.
- **Discovery-first, adversarial analysis at authoring (chosen).** Elevate
  authoring-time adversarial analysis to the everyday, primary mechanism; demote
  the gate to opt-in hardening.
- **Prose/judgment adversarial pass vs. pre-encoded invariants.** The adversarial
  pass is prose/LLM judgment over retrieved neighbours requiring NO pre-encoded
  invariants — chosen as the right tool (broad, low-burden, fires on the authoring
  event where the human judgment already lives), not a compromise.
- **Generalized kind-extensible context vs. a single-purpose decisions tool.**
  Founded as a generalized knowledge context exposing typed accessors per
  knowledge KIND, so later kinds slot in without re-founding the BC.

**On who owns the artifact type system (from PDR-032):**

- **Option A — shopsystem-templates owns it.** Rejected: organizes by medium, not
  meaning; the "template" name describes packaging, not domain; entrenches the
  misnomer.
- **Option B — shopsystem-scenarios owns it.** Rejected: stretches
  scenario-contract semantics into generic document linting; the gate's domain is
  the cross-artifact graph, of which feature files are one node type.
- **Option C — shopsystem-knowledge owns it, absorbing the richer taxonomy
  (chosen).** The artifact corpus — what kinds of knowledge exist, their shapes,
  links, and integrity — IS a knowledge domain, compatible with the discovery-first
  model. Of the reconciliation sub-options, **ALIGN** (force the shapes into one
  field) discards real value and **SUPERSEDE** (retire the pinned coverage) inverts
  the discovery-first reframe; **EXTEND** — grow the model to absorb the net-new
  taxonomy — was the option chosen, and this consolidation now states that grown
  model as one integrated whole rather than as a base plus an extension.

## Decision

`shopsystem-knowledge` is a **kind-extensible, discovery-first knowledge context
that also OWNS the artifact type system and coherence gate**. Concretely:

1. **Founded as a bounded context — kind-extensible, not a single-purpose tool.**
   It exposes typed accessors per knowledge KIND. Its first and only v1 kind is
   `architecture-decision` (the ADR/PDR/brief corpus). Requesting an unregistered
   kind returns a definite "kind not registered" answer, never a silent default.

2. **Discovery-first is the PRIMARY, everyday capability.** At authoring time the
   system retrieves the relevant existing neighbours for a draft via a bounded
   index (relevance triage, not the whole corpus dumped into context), and an
   adversarial pass (prose/LLM judgment over those neighbours) answers three
   questions with a citation to each neighbour: **Covered?** (already decided
   elsewhere), **Contradicts?** (reverses or conflicts with an active decision),
   **Supersedes?** (replaces a prior decision and therefore owes a supersede edge).
   The output is an assessment report of surfaced neighbours by id with a
   covered/contradicts/supersedes verdict each. This requires NO pre-encoded
   invariants. *(The projection/index generation this consumes must be trustworthy;
   HOW that is achieved — single-source generation, determinism, drift-gating — is
   ADR-059's mechanism, referenced not restated.)*

3. **The coherence gate is opt-in hardening — a typed-edge floor posture, not a
   required discipline.** Cheap, model-free typed-edge graph checks over the
   frontmatter edges (supersession-graph coherence — active-supersedes-active,
   asymmetric/dangling supersede edges, the missing `superseded-by` back-edge
   defect — and dangling edge targets) are the always-on deterministic floor. The
   `governed-delta` invariant tripwire (a claim pins a baseline over a governed
   surface; any drift trips it) is OPT-IN per load-bearing claim only, reserved for
   the few claims that want a *continuous* tripwire catching non-authoring changes
   (the one thing the authoring-time adversarial pass structurally cannot cover).
   Most claims never opt in.

4. **shopsystem-knowledge OWNS the artifact type system and coherence gate.** It
   owns the conventions, the per-type artifact formats, the frontmatter schema, and
   the coherence gate that validates the artifact graph. This is one integrated
   ownership, stated whole.

5. **A single `type` discriminator over the eight artifact types.** The one axis is
   `type` (not a dual `kind`+`type` hierarchy), ranging over `intent-record`,
   `candidate`, `session-record`, `prioritization-record`, `brief`, `pdr`, `adr`,
   `current-state`.

6. **Required frontmatter fields.** Every artifact carries, as required frontmatter,
   at minimum: `type`, `description`, `created`, `updated`, `authors` (alongside
   `id`, `title`, `status`). `description` is **required and KEPT** (it feeds the
   L0 card projection and was pinned by scenario `d121b489919c177e`); the date axis
   is split into `created` + `updated` (creation vs. last-substantive-edit); and
   `authors` is required.

7. **Kind-extensibility is a v1 requirement of the *shape*, not a v1 deliverable of
   the kinds.** The accessor contract, the frontmatter schema, and the
   projection/index surface are parameterized by `kind` — a `kind` field, a per-kind
   source location, a per-kind projection set — such that adding a kind later is
   *registering a kind*, not *forking the tool*. `development-principle`,
   `skill-recipe`, and `experiment-research` are named as future kinds and must not
   be boxed out, but none are built now.

8. **Four boundary rules.** (a) **Roles are behavior** (templates BC), **artifacts
   are knowledge** (knowledge BC), **scenarios are the executable contract**
   (scenarios BC), and **artifact instances stay in each product's lead repo** —
   role templates reference knowledge-BC formats, never the reverse. (b) The gate
   owns the cross-artifact link graph and **calls the scenarios CLI** to enumerate
   features/tags — it does **NOT** reimplement canonicalization or re-hash. (c) The
   knowledge BC owns **shapes and integrity, never artifact instances**, which
   remain each product's lead-repo data. (d) The **pour distributes** knowledge-BC
   assets alongside role templates as a supplier/consumer relationship, both
   components versioning independently in the BOM.

9. **Build through specs.** The productionized BC is built from PO-authored
   scenarios informed by this reframe — NOT by re-speccing the gate-heavy prototype.
   The prototype is reference for mechanics only.

10. **Appetite — the type system covers the FULL corpus (corrected).** The artifact
    type system applies to the **entire** corpus: the legacy `brief`/`pdr`/`adr`
    documents are migrated onto the typed schema. This consolidation **retires**
    PDR-032's now-false exclusion line ("Explicitly OUT of appetite: legacy corpus
    migration") entirely — that migration was ratified as full-corpus appetite by
    **PDR-034**, which this PDR references as the decision that achieved it. There is
    no legacy carve-out from the typed schema in the current product truth.

## Consequences

- **Reading the shopsystem-knowledge product truth is now a single act.** One
  document states what the BC is, what it owns, its discovery-first primary
  capability, its opt-in gate posture, its single `type` taxonomy over eight types,
  its required frontmatter, and its four boundary rules — with no "subtract the stale
  appetite line, cross-reference which half of PDR-031 survives" burden.

- **The type system spans the whole corpus.** With PDR-032's exclusion retired and
  PDR-034 ratifying full-corpus migration, progressive-disclosure and
  citation/retrieval tooling gains a fully consistent corpus to index rather than a
  subset. No legacy segment sits outside the schema.

- **Ownership and boundaries are stable; mechanism lives in the ADR lane.** The BC
  owns shapes and integrity, not instances; the gate calls the scenarios CLI rather
  than re-hashing; role templates reference formats one-directionally; the pour
  distributes assets. HOW the formats are managed to prevent template↔schema drift
  is ADR-059's decision (typedef single source, generated read-only files, `--check`
  drift gate) — a reference from this charter, not content of it.

- **Newcomer legibility and clean recursion.** One BC answers "what is a
  candidate?"; downstream products pin the knowledge component for their own
  artifact system; a single `type` discriminator reads more clearly than a two-level
  hierarchy.

- **Draft status; no live supersede edge while `proposed`.** This PDR is `proposed`.
  The product authority is offline and ratifies on return. Per the
  withhold-the-supersede-edge-while-proposed pattern (PDR-034 lines 137–144, and
  ADR-067's withhold-while-proposed pattern), **this draft carries NO live
  `supersedes` frontmatter edge and flips nothing**: no other document's `status`,
  `superseded-by`, or edges are touched by this draft. The whole-document
  supersession fires AT RATIFICATION exactly as the next section documents.

### Supersession staging (fires AT RATIFICATION, not now)

While this PDR is `proposed` it carries no `supersedes` edge and changes no other
document. When the product authority ratifies it, the following whole-document
supersession lands as one atomic operation:

1. **This PDR declares** `supersedes: [pdr-031, pdr-032]` in its frontmatter.
2. **Both predecessors flip** to `status: superseded` with a matching
   `superseded-by: [pdr-035]` back-edge on each (PDR-031 and PDR-032).
3. **Inbound edges re-point** to `pdr-035`:
   - `adr-059`'s `derives-from: [..., pdr-032]` → `[..., pdr-035]`.
   - `current-state.md`'s `incorporates: [..., pdr-032, ...]` → `[..., pdr-035, ...]`.
     (PDR-031 is **not** in `incorporates` — it was never accepted — so no change is
     needed there.)
4. **All other inbound edges auto-follow.** Per ADR-067 D4.3, `superseded-by`
   resolves **transitively**: any remaining inbound edge pointing at a superseded
   predecessor (e.g. ADR-067's own cross-references, PDR-034's cross-reference to
   PDR-032, PDR-032's `derives-from: pdr-031`) auto-follows to `pdr-035` and need
   **not** be hand-rewritten.
